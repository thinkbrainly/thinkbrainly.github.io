16. How many numbers between 999 and 9999 are divisible by 5 and have no repeated digits?


Answer:

952

Step-by-step explanation:

Divisible by 5 numbers can either be ending with 5 or 0. We take the probability of both cases and then add them up

Since the range is from 999 to 9999, and both these numbers being repeated ones, we can safely assume that all answers will have 4 digits on it.

For the case of 0:

_ _ _ 0

Since we don't like to repeat digits, that means that for the first one, we're gonna be left with the set of numbers 1-9 for the first one. 9 items to choose from.

We pick another one for the second digit. Whatever we picked from the first set can't be on the next one, so we're left with just 8 choices.

Using the same logic, we're gonna be left with 7 choices for the third one.

9 x 8 x 7 = 504 choices for numbers ending in 0.


For the case of 5:

_ _ _ 5

This time, we're excluding 5 from the set of the numbers from 0-9. 9 choices.

For the first digit, we know that we're working on 1000 and above numbers, so we can't use zero. That leaves us with 8 choices for the first digit.

For the second digit, we can now use zero, but not 5. So that's 8 choices again.

For the third one, we can't use neither 5 nor 0. So that leaves us with 7.

8 x 8 x 7 = 448 choices for numbers ending in 5.

Add those up and you'll get 952.

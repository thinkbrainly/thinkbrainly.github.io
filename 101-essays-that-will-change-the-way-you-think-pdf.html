11. Broadcasting 101 Tips on How to Deliver a News Report (Essay)pls I need an answer for this right now ​


Answer:

Introduction

Delivering a news report is a challenging and daunting task that requires professional competence and skill. While not everyone is born to be a news broadcaster, here are 101 tips on how to deliver a news report that are sure to boost confidence, enhance presentation skills and lead to a successful broadcast.

1. Research Your Topic

Before attempting to deliver a news report, ensure you have a thorough understanding of the subject and that you have sufficient knowledge to present it confidently. Gather information from reliable sources and craft your script to ensure accuracy.

2. Stay Neutral

News reports are meant to accurately reflect the facts. Your job is to be impartial and avoid injecting personal biases in the content of your report.

3. Consider Your Delivery

The way a news report is delivered is just as important as the content. Although you cannot control each minor detail of your broadcast, you can control your tone and pace. Speak with confidence and take breathers in between sentences to keep your voice steady throughout the broadcast.

4. Practice Makes Perfect

To appear smooth and professional during your broadcast, adequate practice is required. Make sure to practice by yourself as well as with a partner to get used to speaking out loud and articulate your points clearly.

5. Make Eye Contact

Avoid looking away from the camera while delivering your broadcast. Make eye contact with your viewers to give them an assurance of honesty and clarity.

6. Avoid Too Many Facts

Too much information can be overwhelming for the viewers. Be sure to present the most important facts to the viewers and do not crowd your broadcast with unnecessary data.

7. Express Yourself

News reports are more interesting when delivered with enthusiasm. Don't be afraid to add a touch of personality to your broadcast to keep the audience engaged.

8. Check Your Pronunciation

Since news reports usually involve proper nouns, be sure to check your pronunciation of these words as incorrect pronunciation can create confusion and make your report seem less convincing.

9. Dress for the Occasion

Your clothes also contribute to the overall impression. Make sure to dress professionally, but feel free to express your personal style and add your own unique flair to your look.

10. Use Plenty of Examples

Give your viewers examples to help them understand the issue and get a better grasp of the facts. Examples will make the report more interesting and will also help to better explain the facts.

Conclusion

Delivering a news report requires practice, dedication and skill. By following the 101 tips provided above, you will surely be well on your way to becoming a successful news broadcaster. So don your best suit, take a deep breath, and start your journey towards becoming a professional news broadcaster.

14. 5. Why do you think JPEG, PNG, and PDF are printable file formats yet these can still be uploadedonline? Defend your answer.​


Answer:

JPEG, PNG, and PDF are printable file formats because they are designed to produce high-quality images that can be easily printed. They are widely used in graphic design, printing, and publishing industries. JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) is a lossy compression format that is suitable for photographs and complex images. PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is a lossless compression format that supports transparency and is suitable for images with text or sharp lines. PDF (Portable Document Format) is a versatile file format that can contain both text and images, and is commonly used for documents, brochures, and forms.

Although these file formats are designed for printing, they can still be uploaded online because they are compatible with most web browsers and devices. Many websites and online platforms support these file formats, allowing users to upload and share their documents and images online. For example, PDF files can be uploaded to online forms, JPEG and PNG files can be shared on social media or stored in the cloud. Additionally, these file formats are often smaller in size compared to other printable file formats, which makes them easier to upload and download online. Therefore, while these file formats are primarily used for printing, they can also be used for online purposes because they are widely accepted and supported.

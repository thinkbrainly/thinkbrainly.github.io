23. Joey has.a piece of bond paper measure 12mm by 10mm and he wants to draw a square at the center of the bond paper and cut it out if the side of the square measure 5mm,what is the remaining area of the bond paper ​


Answer:

the remaining area of the bond paper is 95mm^2.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the remaining area of the bond paper, we first need to calculate the area of the square that Joey wants to cut out from the center of the paper.

The area of a square is given by the formula: A = s^2, where s is the length of one side of the square.

In this case, the length of one side of the square is 5mm, so the area of the square is:

A = 5mm x 5mm = 25mm^2

To find the remaining area of the bond paper, we need to subtract the area of the square from the total area of the paper.

The total area of the paper is:

A = length x width = 12mm x 10mm = 120mm^2

So, the remaining area of the bond paper after cutting out the square is:

120mm^2 - 25mm^2 = 95mm^2

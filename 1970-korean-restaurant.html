17. differentiate adolescence in 1970 and today


Adolescence in the 1970s was marked by a more traditional and conservative social and cultural environment. Teenagers were expected to conform to societal norms and were often expected to follow more traditional paths in terms of education, career, and family. The role of technology was limited, and communication was mostly face-to-face or through telephone calls.

In contrast, adolescence today is marked by a much more diverse and interconnected world. Technology has revolutionized the way young people communicate and interact with each other, with social media and the internet playing a major role in shaping their identities and relationships. There is greater acceptance of diversity in terms of gender, sexuality, and culture, and young people are more likely to express themselves in non-traditional ways. There is also a growing emphasis on mental health and well-being, as well as on issues such as climate change and social justice. Overall, adolescence today is marked by a greater sense of individuality, self-expression, and awareness of global issues.

23. what republic act of 1970​


Republic Act No. 6132 was enacted in 1970, which established the National Science Development Board (NSDB). The NSDB aimed to promote the development of science and technology in the Philippines by formulating policies and plans, funding research and development projects, and coordinating with other government agencies and private organizations. The act also created the Science Education Institute (SEI) to provide scholarships and grants to students pursuing science and technology courses.

7. what are the 3 primary prevention of the disease dengue


Answer:

The three primary prevention measures for dengue are:

1. Eliminating breeding sites of mosquitoes: Dengue is transmitted through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes. Therefore, the best way to prevent dengue is to eliminate breeding sites of mosquitoes by removing standing water in and around homes, schools, and other public places.

2. Using mosquito repellent: Mosquito repellents containing DEET, picaridin, IR3535, or oil of lemon eucalyptus can help prevent mosquito bites and reduce the risk of dengue infection.

3. Wearing protective clothing: Covering the skin with long-sleeved shirts, pants, and socks can reduce the risk of mosquito bites and prevent dengue infection.

26. dengue fever primary prevention​


Answer:

Primary prevention of dengue mainly resides in eliminating or reducing the mosquito vector for dengue. Public spraying for mosquitoes is the most important aspect of this vector. Application of larvicides such as Abate® to standing water is more effective in the long term control of mosquitoes.

Primary prevention which includes use of mosquito repellents, mosquito bed nets, mosquito coils, protective clothing and regularly removing sources of stagnant water to prevent mosquito breeding is suggested as the most effective measure in dengue prevention and control

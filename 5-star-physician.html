There are different types of physician. They are categorized based on their specialization. Based on the pictures, below are the explanation of different types of physician.

1. Cardiologists

Cardiologists specialize in the diagnosis, treatment, and management of cardiovascular disorders, such as arrhythmias, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. Despite the fact that they are medical specialists, they may concentrate on interventional procedures in their practice.

2. Pediatricians

Doctors that specialize in treating children, adolescents, and infants diagnose and treat a wide range of illnesses and injuries. Pediatricians diagnose common children illnesses like asthma, allergies, and croup in addition to practicing preventive medicine.

3. Obstetricians and gynecologists

Women's reproductive systems are treated medically by obstetricians and gynecologists (OB/GYNs).

4. Pulmonologist

A pulmonologist is a physician who specializes in the respiratory system.

5. Geriatricians

Geriatricians are primary care doctors who have additional training in treating older adults, especially those 65 and up.

6. Neurologist

Neurologists are physicians who diagnose and treat diseases of the nervous system, brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves, muscles, and blood vessels.

7. Urologists

A urologist is a physician who focuses on disorders of the reproductive system and conditions that affect men, women, and children's urinary tracts.

8. Dermatologist

Dermatologists are expert medical doctors and skin surgeons with the unique skills and experience to offer the best care for the organ that cares for you.

9. Opthalmologist

An ophthalmologist is a medical or osteopathic doctor who specializes in eye and vision care.

10. Gastroenterologist

A gastroenterologist is a doctor who treats digestive disorders.

10. In a hospital, there are 10 nurses and 5 physicians. Seven nurses and two physicians are males. The sample space can be written in a table form. Staff Females Males Total Nurses: (3 , 7, 10) Physicians: (3, 2, 5) total : If a person is selected from the staff, what is the probability that a person is nurse of female? What is the probability that a person selected is a male or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is male or female? What is the probability that a person selected is nurse or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is a nurse or male?


Answer:

If we represent the events "Nurse" and "Female" as N and F respectively, then we can fill in the table as follows:

Staff Females Males Total

Nurses (N) 3 7 10

Physicians (P) 2 3 5

Total 5 10 15

The probability that a person selected from the staff is a nurse or female is:

P(N or F) = P(N) + P(F) - P(N and F) = 10/15 + 5/15 - 3/15 = 12/15 = 4/5

The probability that a person selected is a male or physician is:

P(M or P) = P(M) + P(P) - P(M and P) = 8/15 + 5/15 - 2/15 = 11/15

The probability that a person selected is male or female is equal to 1 because every person is either male or female.

The probability that a person selected is a nurse or physician is:

P(N or P) = P(N) + P(P) - P(N and P) = 10/15 + 5/15 - 2/15 = 13/15

The probability that a person selected is a nurse or male is:

P(N or M) = P(N) + P(M) - P(N and M) = 10/15 + 8/15 - 7/15 = 11/15

28. In a hospital, there are 10 nurses and 5 physicians. Seven nurses and two physicians are males. The sample space can be written in a table form. Staff Females Males Total Nurses: (3 , 7, 10) Physicians: (3, 2, 5) total : If a person is selected from the staff, what is the probability that a person is nurse of female? What is the probability that a person selected is a male or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is male or female? What is the probability that a person selected is nurse or physician? What is the probability that a person selected is a nurse or male?


The sample space can be written as follows:

Staff Females Males Total
Nurses: 3 7 10
Physicians: 2 3 5
Total: 5 10 15

The probability that a person selected is a nurse of female is:
P(nurse or female) = P(nurse and female)/P(total)
P(nurse and female) = 3/15
P(total) = 15/15
Therefore, P(nurse or female) = (3/15)/(15/15) = 3/15 = 1/5 = 0.2

The probability that a person selected is a male or physician is:
P(male or physician) = P(male) + P(physician) - P(male and physician)
P(male) = 10/15
P(physician) = 5/15
P(male and physician) = 2/15
Therefore, P(male or physician) = (10/15) + (5/15) - (2/15) = 13/15 = 0.87

The probability that a person selected is male or female is:
P(male or female) = P(male) + P(female) - P(male and female)
P(female) = 5/15
P(male and female) = 7/15
Therefore, P(male or female) = (10/15) + (5/15) - (7/15) = 8/15 = 0.53

The probability that a person selected is a nurse or physician is:
P(nurse or physician) = P(nurse) + P(physician) - P(nurse and physician)
P(nurse) = 10/15
P(nurse and physician) = 2/15
Therefore, P(nurse or physician) = (10/15) + (5/15) - (2/15) = 13/15 = 0.87

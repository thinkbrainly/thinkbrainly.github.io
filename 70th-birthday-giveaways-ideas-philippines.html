15. Find the 70th term of the arithmetic equence -6, 5, 16,. −6,5,16.


To find the 70th term of an arithmetic sequence, we first need to find the common difference between consecutive terms. In this case, the common difference is 16 - 5 = 11.

To find the 70th term, we can use the formula for the nth term of an arithmetic sequence: a_n = a_1 + (n - 1)d, where a_n is the nth term, a_1 is the first term, and d is the common difference.

Substituting the values from the given sequence, we get:

a_70 = -6 + (70 - 1) * 11 = -6 + 69 * 11 = -6 + 759 = 753

Therefore, the 70th term of the arithmetic sequence -6, 5, 16, ... is 753.

25. Rochelle got a score of 55 which is equivalent to 70th percentile in a mathematics test. Which of the following is NOT true?


Percentile:

Given that Rochelle got a score of 55 which is equivalent to 70th percentile in a Mathematics test. Then, it is not true that her score is below the 5th decile.

To prove that the statement is incorrect, consider the value of the 5th decile and 70th decile. Decile means the score points that divide the distribution into 10 equal parts. They are often denoted by D. On the other hand, percentile is are the score points which divide a distribution into one hundred equal parts, so that each part represents the data set. It is often denoted by P.

Given that Rochelle's score is in the 70th percentile and comparing decile with percentile will give: 1st decile = 10th percentile. It follows that the 5th decile = 50th percentile. But Rochelle's score is the 70th percentile which is greater than the 50th percentile or the 5th decile. Therefore, her score of 55 does not belong to the scores below the 5th decile.

However, it can be said that Rochelle's score is above 70% of her classmates and 30% of their class got a score of 55 and above. Likewise, if the passing mark will be based on the first quartile, then Rochelle had passed the Mathematics test.

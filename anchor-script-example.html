3. broadcasting script about weather and climate change we have anchored ,local ,and.. ​


Introduction:

Anchor 1: Good day, viewers. Welcome to our special broadcast on weather and climate change. I'm [Name of Anchor 1] and I'm here with [Name of Anchor 2] and [Name of Anchor 3] to give you the latest updates on the weather situation in our area and discuss the issue of climate change.

Local Weather Update:

Anchor 2: Let's start with the local weather update. According to the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration or PAGASA, our area is currently experiencing [weather condition]. The temperature is at [temperature] degrees Celsius with [wind direction and speed].

Anchor 3: We advise everyone to stay updated on the latest weather advisories from PAGASA and to take necessary precautions, especially during severe weather conditions.

Climate Change Discussion:

Anchor 1: Climate change is a pressing issue that affects not just our country, but the entire world. It's a complex issue that involves the warming of the Earth's atmosphere and its impact on our environment and daily lives.

Anchor 2: Studies show that the increase in global temperatures is causing more extreme weather events, such as typhoons, floods, and droughts. This is because the warming atmosphere increases the amount of water vapor, which leads to more intense rainfall and longer dry periods.

Anchor 3: Climate change also affects our health, agriculture, and economy. It can lead to the spread of diseases, damage crops, and cause a decline in fish populations, affecting fishermen and the fishing industry.

Local Initiatives:

Anchor 1: It's important for us to take action to address climate change. There are local initiatives that aim to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, promote renewable energy, and increase the resilience of communities to the impacts of climate change.

Anchor 2: [Name of local initiative] is one example of a program that aims to reduce carbon emissions in our area by [brief description of program].

Anchor 3: It's important for us to support these initiatives and do our part in reducing our carbon footprint, such as by reducing our use of plastic, conserving energy, and using public transportation or carpooling.

Conclusion:

Anchor 1: That's it for our special broadcast on weather and climate change. We hope this has given you a better understanding of the current weather situation in our area and the issue of climate change.

Anchor 2: Remember to always stay informed and take necessary precautions during severe weather conditions.

Anchor 3: Let's all do our part in preserving our environment and protecting our planet. Thank you for watching and good day.

8. example of Talkshow script ​


Answer:

Announcer: Welcome to "Good Morning, America!" Today, we have an exciting show planned for you. We'll be talking to an Olympic gold medalist, an inspiring entrepreneur, and a celebrity chef. All that and more coming up.

Host: Good morning, everyone! It's time for a new episode of "Good Morning, America." I'm your host, John Smith, and I'm here to bring you the latest news and stories from around the world.

First up, we have Olympic gold medalist Michael Phelps. Michael, you've achieved so much in your career. What advice would you give to young people who want to reach the same level of success as you?

Michael Phelps: Well, the most important thing is to never give up. No matter how difficult the journey may be, keep pushing forward and don't let anything stand in your way. Believe in yourself and you'll make it to the top!

Host: Great advice! Thanks for joining us, Michael.

Next up, we have entrepreneur and philanthropist, Steve Jobs. Steve, you've done so much for the world of business and technology. What advice would you give to entrepreneurs who are just starting out?

Steve Jobs: The most important thing is to stay focused and believe in yourself. Don't be afraid to take risks and don't be afraid to fail. Success comes from learning from your mistakes.

Host: Thanks for that great advice, Steve.

Finally, we have celebrity chef Emeril Lagasse. Emeril, what's the best way to become a successful chef?

Emeril Lagasse: The best way to become a successful chef is to practice, practice, practice. You have to be passionate about food and cooking and never stop learning. Stay up to date on the latest trends and techniques, and always be willing to experiment with new flavors and ingredients.

Host: That's great advice! Thanks for joining us, Emeril.

Well, that's all the time we have for today. Thank you for watching "Good Morning, America." We'll see you next time with more inspiring stories and guests!

15. Give an example of positive scripting


Answer:

Instead of…

“I’m afraid that isn’t included in your policy…”

Say…“Your policy doesn’t cover that, but I can check if we can offer that to you in a different package.”

Do say:

Hi, my name is X. May I ask your name?

Great to meet you, Y. How can I assist you today?

How can I help you?

What can I help you with today?

Don’t say:

What is wrong?

Are you unsatisfied?

Are you confused?

What’s your problem?

17. anchoring bias example in your experience?​


Anchoring bias is a cognitive bias that causes us to rely too heavily on the first piece of information we are given about a topic. When we are setting plans or making estimates about something, we interpret newer information from the reference point of our anchor, instead of seeing it objectively.

30. anchoring bias example in your experience?​


Answer:

When people are trying to make a decision, they often use an anchor or focal point as a reference or starting point. Psychologists have found that people have a tendency to rely too heavily on the very first piece of information they learn, which can have a serious impact on the decision they end up making.1﻿ In psychology, this type of cognitive bias is known as the anchoring bias or anchoring effect.

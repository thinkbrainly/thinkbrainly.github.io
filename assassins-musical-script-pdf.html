6. Create a very short script of a possible musical play in your own dialect.


Title: Ang Pagbabago ng Panahon (The Changing of Seasons)

Characters:

Jessa - a young girl who loves the rainy season

Juan - Jessa's friend who loves the summer season

Mr. Sun - a personification of the sun

Ms. Rain - a personification of the rain

Scene 1:

(Jessa and Juan are playing under the sun)

Jessa: (singing) Oh, ang init-init ng araw, gusto ko ng ulan!

Juan: (singing) Hindi ko gusto ang ulan, mas gusto ko ang araw!

Mr. Sun: (appears on stage) Anong nangyayari sa inyo? Hindi ba kayo nag-eenjoy sa panahon ninyo?

Jessa: Ayoko na ng araw, gusto ko ng ulan!

Juan: Ayoko ng ulan, gusto ko ng araw!

Ms. Rain: (appears on stage) Bakit hindi niyo subukan ang panahon ng kabila? Baka doon kayo mag-enjoy.

Scene 2:

(Jessa and Juan are now experiencing the rainy season)

Jessa: (singing) Ang lamig-lamig ng ulan, mas gusto ko ito!

Juan: (singing) Ang tagaktak ng ulan, hindi ko kaya ito!

Ms. Rain: (appears on stage) Ayos ba ang panahon ninyo ngayon?

Jessa: Sobrang ayos! Gusto ko na ng palaging ulan!

Juan: Hindi ko kaya! Gusto ko nang bumalik sa araw!

Mr. Sun: (appears on stage) At bakit hindi niyo subukan ang panahon ko ulit?

Jessa: Sige nga, subukan natin ulit!

Scene 3:

(Jessa and Juan are now experiencing the summer season)

Juan: (singing) Ang init-init ng araw, sobra na ito!

Jessa: (singing) Hindi ko kaya ito, gusto ko na ng ulan!

Mr. Sun: (appears on stage) Tama na ang kakulitan ninyo, mag-enjoy na lang kayo sa panahon ninyo ngayon!

Ms. Rain: (appears on stage) Tama si Mr. Sun, hindi natin kailangan mag-away dahil magkaiba tayo ng panahon. Ang importante ay mag-enjoy tayo sa bawat panahon na ating nararanasan.

Jessa: Tama ka, Ms. Rain. Mag-enjoy na lang tayo sa bawat panahon!

Juan: Oo nga, basta't magkasama tayo, masaya na ako kahit anong panahon pa ang ating nararanasan.

(All characters sing together as the stage fades to black)

End of play.

16. 2.ASSASSINATION OF GOVERNMENT BUSTAMANTE​


Answer:

Following a 1644 inquiry into the lost of "Nuestra Senora de la Concepcion," a Spanish galleon plying the lucrative trade route between Manila and Acapulco, Spanish officials charged that Manila's colonial governor, Don Sebastian Hurtado de Corcuera, had misappropriated treasures in the Philippines and was shipping them back to Spain as personal cargo. Among the 59 charges brought against Corcuera was the accusation that he shipped personal loot treasures of gold and jewelry procured as bribes for granting special favors and appointments. He was also charged with appointing his nephew, Don Juan Francisco, to command the ship to protect the governor's spoils.

The Manila galleon trade was one of the most constant but dangerous and yet profitable commercial enterprises in Mexico and European colonial history. Between 1565 and 1815 it carried the treasures of the Orient via Acapulco, Mexico in exchange for New World silver and the manufactured goods of Europe. One galleon a year would embark on the trip. More than 40 galleons were lost in the treacherous seas and looted by pirates over the centuries.

20. Was benigno aquino executed or assassination


Answer:

assassination

Explanation:

Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., a former Philippine senator, was assassinated on Sunday, August 21, 1983, on the tarmac of Manila International Airport (now named Ninoy Aquino International Airport in his honor). A longtime political opponent of President Ferdinand Marcos, Aquino had just landed in his home country after three years of self-imposed exile in the United States when he was shot in the head while being escorted from an aircraft to a vehicle that was waiting to transport him to prison. Also killed was Rolando Galman, who was later implicated in Aquino's murder.

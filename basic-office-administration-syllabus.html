1. As an office professional, explain the importance of learning the basic duties and responsibilities of administrative office professionals.Pa help po plss​


Answer:

As an office professional, it is important to have a strong understanding of the basic duties and responsibilities of administrative office professionals in order to effectively perform your job and contribute to the overall success of the office.

Some of the key responsibilities of administrative office professionals include:

Managing and organizing office tasks and processes

Scheduling appointments and meetings

Answering phone calls and emails

Greeting and directing visitors

Handling correspondence and mail

Creating and maintaining office records and files

Managing office supplies and equipment

Supporting other office staff in completing their tasks

Performing data entry and other computer-based tasks

Providing administrative support to management or other team members

By having a strong understanding of these basic duties and responsibilities, you can ensure that you are able to perform your job effectively and efficiently, and that you are able to support your colleagues and contribute to the overall success of the office.

Additionally, having a good knowledge of the basic office procedures, time management, and communication skills will help you to become a valuable asset to any organization.

by: Jehanne Dubrow

Throughout this course,

we’ll study the American

landscape of our yard, coiled line

of the garden hose,

muddy furrows in the grass

awaiting our analysis,

what’s called close reading

of the ground. And somewhere

something will yip in pain

perhaps, a paw caught in a wire,

or else the furred and oily

yowling of desire.

And flickering beyond the fence,

we’ll see the slatted lives

of strangers. The light

above a neighbor’s porch

will be a test of how we tolerate

the half-illumination

of uncertainty, a glow

that’s argument to shadow.

Or if not that, we’ll write an essay

on the stutter of the bulb,

the little glimmering that goes

before the absolute of night.

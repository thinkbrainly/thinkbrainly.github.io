10. During a rescue operation, two ships were sent by the coast guards to rescue the passengers trapped in a lifeboat. To make the rescue operation easier, a rectangular coordinate system was used to map the entire rescue location. Ship A has coordinates (-1, -2) and ship 8 has coordinates (-4, 11. Both ships have spotlights fixated on the same lifeboat. The light beam from ship Atravels along the line y=2x The light beam from ship 8travels along the line y=x+5. As the coast guard personnel on duty, you are tasked to determine the coordinates of the lifeboat to guide the ships. You are to submit to the team leader of the rescue operation an accurate and properly labeled sketch that shows the coordinates of the two ships and the lifeboat. ​


Answer:

To determine the coordinates of the lifeboat using the information given, we need to find the point of intersection between the two lines representing the light beams from the two ships.

The equation for the line from Ship A is y = 2x.

The equation for the line from Ship B is y = x + 5.

To find the point of intersection, we can set the two equations equal to each other and solve for x and y.

y = 2x

y = x + 5

x + 5 = 2x

x = 5

Substituting this value of x back into the first equation, we get:

y = 2(5)

y = 10

Therefore, the coordinates of the lifeboat are (5, 10).

To create a labeled sketch, you would need to plot the coordinates of the two ships and the lifeboat on a rectangular coordinate system and label them accordingly

17. what is the service air system of the main engine of the ship​


Answer:

Compressed air on ships is used for different purposes. High-pressure air of 30 bar is mainly used for the main engine starting.

This high-pressure air is reduced to lower working pressures through pressure reduction valves and is used for other important purposes.

The air at reduced pressures of 7-8 bar is used as service air for a number of applications.

30. 4. Why General service pump is interconnected to all other ship’s pipeline system such as freshwatersystem, ballast system, and other piping system on ship’s? ​


Bilge and ballast systems are interconnected so that each can perform the other's function in an emergency, ie a ballast pump could be used to pump out a flooded engine room. ... In passenger and cargo ships where the engine room provides bilge pumping, the whole ship is the 'enclosed space'.

Answer:

The various pumps and lines are interconnected to some extent so that each pump can act as an alternative or standby for another. The capacity of a bilge system is defined by the diameter of the bilge main and pump capacity for the volume of the enclosed space.

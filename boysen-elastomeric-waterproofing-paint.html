5. layer of cells on leaves that is waterproof


A plant cuticle is a protecting film covering the epidermis of leaves, young shoots and other aerial plant organs without periderm. This cuticle helps seal in the water, making the leaves virtually waterproof. It consists of lipid and hydrocarbon polymers impregnated with wax, and is synthesized exclusively by the epidermal cells.


6. 3. Why lipids are "waterproof"?​


Answer:

Lipids are non-polar molecules, so they are not soluble in polar solvents like water. If you've ever tried to combine oil and water, you've seen how poorly they mix. This feature of lipids allows our bodies to use them as waterproof barriers and as biological membranes.

The solution is very easy, namely to check first whether the old paint layer has completely dried before applying the new coating.

13. above 9.The great way to enhance and protect projects from scratches, stains, water damage, and general wear and tear. a. painting b. waterproofing c. cleaning d. none of the above​


Explanation:

b. Waterproofing.

Waterproofing is the process of making a surface or structure water-resistant, thus preventing water damage or penetration. It is a great way to enhance and protect projects from scratches, stains, water damage, and general wear and tear. This is especially important for projects that are exposed to moisture or water, such as outdoor decks, roofs, and foundations. By waterproofing, you can prolong the life of your project and save money on repairs or replacements in the long run.

23. what is waterproof feathers ​

24. In the school election Javed got 22% of the votes. Nathan got 30% of the vote and beat Javed by 28 votes. How many pupils voted in the election?


Answer:

350 pupils

Step-by-step explanation:

In mathematics, percentage commonly use in a rate of base amount and/or number. It is a ratio express in fraction of a number that can be written as both a decimal and a fraction. For example, one percent is one hundredth of a whole which is 1%, 1/100, or 0.01.

In the given problem, since the data is 22% of the votes from Javed, Nathan got 30% of the vote and beat Javed by 28 votes. It indicates that 30%-22% is equal to the percentage of 28 votes from the total pupils voted.

Thus 30-22= 8%, 8% of x is 28, where x is the total number of pupils who vote

28= 8%(x)

x= 28/8%

x= 28/0.08

x= 350

Therefore, there are 350 pupils voted in school election.

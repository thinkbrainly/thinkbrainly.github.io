10. What is a sudden, calamitous event that brings damage, loss, destruction and devastation of life and property?


The sudden, calamitous event that brings damage, loss, destruction, and devastation of life and property is what we called disaster. Anyone can be at danger, including survivors residing in the affected cities and even those who are in rescue operations. There are two types of disasters, natural and man-made disaster.

Natural Disasters are severe, unexpected events produced by environmental circumstances that possibly harm people and damage property.  

Here are some examples of Natural Disasters:

Earthquakes Landslides Tsunamis Volcanic activity Avalanches  Typhoon / Hurricane Floods Wildfires Storms Wave surges Disease epidemics  

Man-made disasters are unfortunate incidents caused by human actions that can create harm to people, animals, and damage to property.

Here are some examples of Man-made Disasters:

Transport accidents Industrial accidents Shootings Explosions Acts of terrorism Fires Chemical Spills

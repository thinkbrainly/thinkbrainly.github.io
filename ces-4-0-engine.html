4. Suppose ABCD is a parallelogram and the diagonals meet at E. If AE = 3x-44 and CE=x-4, find the value of x, and the lengths of AE, CE, and AC.​


pa brainliest po

Since ABCD is a parallelogram, its opposite sides are parallel and equal in length. Therefore, we have:

AB = CD

BC = AD

We also know that the diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other. Therefore, we have:

AE = EC

BE = ED

We are given that AE = 3x-44 and EC = x-4. Therefore, we can set these two expressions equal to each other:

3x-44 = x-4

Simplifying this equation, we get:

2x = 40

x = 20

Therefore, we know that AE = EC = 3x-44 = x-4 = 3(20)-44 = -4, and we can find the length of AC as follows:

AC = AB + BC

Since ABCD is a parallelogram, we know that AB is parallel to DC and BC is parallel to AD. Therefore, we can extend AE and EC to intersect DC and AD, respectively, at points F and G. Then, we have:

AF = AE = -4

FC = EC = -4

DG = EC = -4

GB = AE = -4

Since AF = GB and FC = DG, we know that the segments AFGB and FCDG form two parallelograms. Therefore, we have:

AC = AB + BC = FG = CD = 2(-4) = -8

Therefore, the value of x is 20, and the lengths of AE, CE, AC are all -4.

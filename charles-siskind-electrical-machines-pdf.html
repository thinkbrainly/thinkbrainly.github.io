16. Activity 3: Define the terminology below 1. Charles Coulomb 2. Atom 3. Electricity 4. Magnetism 5. Proton 6. Electron 7. Neutron 8. Electric charge 9. Conduction 10. Electrostatic ​


1. Charles Coulomb: Charles-Augustin de Coulomb was a French physicist who developed the law of electrostatic attraction and repulsion, known as Coulomb's law.

2. Atom: An atom is the basic unit of matter that consists of a nucleus made up of protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons that orbit the nucleus.

3. Electricity: Electricity refers to the flow of electrons or electrically charged particles through a conductor or other medium.

4. Magnetism: Magnetism is a property of certain materials that causes them to attract or repel other materials based on their magnetic fields.

5. Proton: A proton is a positively charged subatomic particle found in the nucleus of an atom.

6. Electron: An electron is a negatively charged subatomic particle found orbiting the nucleus of an atom.

7. Neutron: A neutron is an uncharged subatomic particle found in the nucleus of an atom.

8. Electric charge: Electric charge refers to the fundamental property of matter that causes it to attract or repel other charged objects based on the polarity of their charges.

9. Conduction: Conduction refers to the transfer of heat, electricity, or sound through a material or medium.

10. Electrostatics: Electrostatics refers to the study of electrically charged objects at rest, including the behavior of electric fields and the interaction between charged particles.

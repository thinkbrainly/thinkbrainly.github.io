1. 1.The president of a university would like to know the average commuting time of students when travelling to school. To do this, he asked a researcher to gather data from 100 students. The sample mean was found to be 34.05 minutes and the standard deviation to be 9.50 minutes. At a confidence level of 90 percent, what is the confidence interval that can estimate the population mean?2. Edward owns a meat store, and he wants to find out the average amount of his daily sale. He randomly recorded his sales for 16 days and found the average to be Php8,500. It is known from last year’s record that the standard deviation of his sales is Php150, and a 99% level of confidence is to be used. Given a sample size of 125, find the length of confidence interval.3.Find the 90th percentile of the t-distribution if the sample size is 25. patulong po with solutions po sana kung pa sya na i solve, ty!!​

Step-by-step explanation:

1. To find the confidence interval that can estimate the population mean, we can use the formula:

Confidence Interval = Sample Mean ± (Z-score) x (Standard Deviation / Square Root of Sample Size)

where Z-score is the critical value from the standard normal distribution corresponding to the desired confidence level. For a 90% confidence level, the Z-score is 1.645.

Substituting the given values, we get:

Confidence Interval = 34.05 ± (1.645) x (9.50 / √100)

Confidence Interval = 34.05 ± (1.645) x (0.95)

Confidence Interval = 34.05 ± 1.56

Confidence Interval = (32.49, 35.61)

Therefore, we can say with 90% confidence that the average commuting time of students when travelling to school is between 32.49 to 35.61 minutes.

2. To find the length of confidence interval, we can use the formula:

Length of Confidence Interval = (Z-score) x (Standard Deviation / Square Root of Sample Size)

where Z-score is the critical value from the standard normal distribution corresponding to the desired confidence level. For a 99% confidence level, the Z-score is 2.576.

Substituting the given values, we get:

Length of Confidence Interval = (2.576) x (150 / √125)

Length of Confidence Interval = 47.45

Therefore, the length of the confidence interval is Php 47.45. This means that we can be 99% confident that the true population mean of Edward's daily sales is within ± Php 47.45 of the sample mean of Php 8,500.

3. The 90th percentile of the t-distribution with a sample size of 25 can be found using a t-table or a calculator. Using a t-table with 24 degrees of freedom (since sample size is 25), we can find the value corresponding to the area of 0.90 (since we want the 90th percentile). The value is approximately 1.318.

Therefore, the 90th percentile of the t-distribution with a sample size of 25 is approximately 1.318.

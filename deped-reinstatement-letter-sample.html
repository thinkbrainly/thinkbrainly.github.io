15. Directions: Read the following scenario and instructions. President for a Day! Scenario: As the newly elected Supreme Student Government (SSG) President, you are given a chance to write an open letter, essay to DepEd Secretary Briones. In your letter you write the challenges that you encounter as the face-to-face classes have resumed last year and write down suggestions that you want to share with the DepEd Secretary.​


Dear Secretary Briones,

As the newly elected Supreme Student Government President, I write to you today to express the challenges that we, the students, encounter as the face-to-face classes have resumed last year.

Since the reopening of schools, we have been struggling to cope with the current academic setup. The ongoing pandemic has made it difficult for us to adjust to the new normal. We find it hard to keep up with the pressure of online classes, especially with limited access to technology and internet connection. Moreover, we also encounter difficulties in adjusting to the face-to-face classes, as the virus continues to pose a threat to our health and safety.

As such, I would like to suggest some recommendations that may help address these challenges:

1. Ensure that all students have access to technology and a stable internet connection. Many of us come from underprivileged families who cannot afford to buy laptops or pay for monthly internet subscriptions. Providing us with the necessary gadgets and internet access can help us participate in online classes and complete our assignments.

2. Implement flexible schedules. With the ongoing pandemic, some students may still be hesitant to attend face-to-face classes. Providing alternative schedules, such as blended learning, can help students feel more comfortable and safe.

3. Conduct regular testing and vaccination. It is essential to prioritize the health and safety of the students and teachers. Regular testing can help identify possible cases of COVID-19, and vaccination can help prevent the spread of the virus.

4. Provide mental health support. The pandemic has taken a toll on everyone's mental health, especially for students who are dealing with the academic pressure and uncertainties brought about by the pandemic. Providing counseling services and mental health support can help us cope with these challenges.

As we continue to navigate the challenges brought about by the pandemic, we need the support of the government and education sector to ensure that we receive quality education and promote our well-being.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]


16. summarize the sample news report three to five sentences news report-deped sets opening of school year 2022-2023 on August 22​


Answer:

The Department of Education (DepEd) on Friday said it is ready for the scheduled opening of classes on Aug. 22.

"All systems go na tayo for the opening of classes," DepEd spokesperson Michael Poa said in a press conference.

Poa said a total of 24,175 public and private schools nationwide or 46 percent will implement five-day face-to-face classes, while 29,721 schools or about 51.8 percent will implement blended learning modality.

Meanwhile, the remaining 1.29 percent or about 1,004 schools will conduct distance learning.

Poa, citing Aug. 19 DepEd data, said the current total enrollment on the basic education level is 27 million learners, which is 96 percent of the agency's target of 28.6 million.

DepEd Undersecretary Epimaco Densing III earlier said schools will be given options of in-person, blended, or full-distance learning only until Oct. 31.

After this, all enrolled students will be required to attend face-to-face classes, under DepEd Order (DO) 034, series of 2022.

"This is mandatory, they are all required to go to school and the mandate is really to eventually go face-to-face. Everybody who's enrolled should abide by the Department Order, parents are also encouraged to ensure that their students go to school. Again, we're trying to do some educational recovery," he said.

According to the same DO 034, there are 203 days for Academic Year 2022-2023 for public schools, which will begin on Aug. 22, 2022 and will end on July 7, 2023.

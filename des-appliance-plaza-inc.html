8. RESEARCH FOR GRADE 10 (review of related literature) titled “Attractiveness of Incentive Tools Influencing the Performance and Motivation of Teachers in St. Louse de Marillac College of Sorsogon, Inc., Sorsogon City”


Answer:

Introduction:

Incentive tools are often used by organizations to motivate their employees to perform better. In the context of educational institutions, teachers are the most important assets as they are responsible for imparting knowledge and skills to students. The purpose of this review of related literature is to explore the attractiveness of incentive tools that influence the performance and motivation of teachers in St. Louse de Marillac College of Sorsogon, Inc., Sorsogon City.

Incentive Tools:

Incentive tools refer to the rewards or benefits offered to employees for their performance. In the context of educational institutions, incentive tools can include monetary rewards, promotions, recognition, and professional development opportunities. These tools are designed to motivate employees to perform better, improve their productivity, and enhance their job satisfaction.

Performance and Motivation:

Performance and motivation are closely related concepts. A motivated teacher is more likely to perform better than an unmotivated one. Motivation can be intrinsic or extrinsic. Intrinsic motivation refers to the internal drive to perform well, while extrinsic motivation refers to external rewards that motivate an individual to perform better. Incentive tools can be used to enhance both intrinsic and extrinsic motivation.

Attractiveness of Incentive Tools:

The attractiveness of incentive tools is influenced by several factors. One of the most important factors is the perceived value of the incentive. If the incentive is perceived as valuable, it is more likely to motivate teachers to perform better. Another important factor is the fairness of the incentive. If the incentive is perceived as fair, it is more likely to be attractive to teachers.

Research on Incentive Tools:

Several studies have been conducted on the effectiveness of incentive tools in motivating teachers. According to a study by Rizvi and Naveed (2012), monetary incentives are the most effective tool for motivating teachers. Another study by Kim et al. (2016) found that non-monetary incentives, such as recognition and professional development opportunities, are also effective in motivating teachers.

Conclusion:

Incentive tools are important in motivating teachers to perform better. The attractiveness of these tools is influenced by several factors, including the perceived value and fairness of the incentive. Monetary incentives are the most effective tool for motivating teachers, but non-monetary incentives can also be effective. Educational institutions should carefully design and implement incentive programs to ensure that they are attractive and effective in enhancing the performance and motivation of teachers.

17. Elise walks diagonally from one corner of a square plaza to another. Each side of the plaza is 50 meters. What is the diagonal distance across the plaza?


Answer:

50√2 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Method 1: We know that the side of the plaza is 50 meters. Since, it is a square then we can use the pythagorean theorem.

So, a² + b² = c²

Since and a and b are the legs which are 50 meters, we are solving for c the hypotenuse or in this case, the diagonal.

(50)² + (50)² = c²

2500 + 2500 = c²

5000 = c²

c = √5000

c = √100 x 25 x 2

c = 50√2 cm

Method 2: Using a formula for solving the diagonal of a square without the pythagorean theorem is:

Diagonal of a Square = s√2

where s = side length

Given, s = 50 therefore it is simply 50√2 cm.

22. Elise walks diagonally from one corner of a square plaza to another. Each side of the plaza is 50 meters. What is the diagonal distance across the plaza??? Khan Academy question


Answer:

The diagonal distance is 50√2 meters or 71 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Use Pythagorean Theorem to  solve for the length of the diagonal where diagonal is equal to hypotenuse.

The square of hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of its two other sides.

(diagonal)² = (50)² + (50)²

(diagonal)² = 2(50)²

diagonal = √2(50)²

diagonal = 50√2  meters

diagonal ≈  70.71  or  71 meters

Or use the Right Isosceles Triangle Theorem / 45°-45°-90° where the hypotenuse (in this case, diagonal) is equal to side√2.

Therefore:

Side of the square = 50 meters

Diagonal/Hypotenuse = 50√2 meters

•NAME OF APPLIANCE/S SAFETY DEVICE•

"CIRCUIT BREAKER"

•HOW IT FUNCTION•

A circuit breaker is a safety device to prevent damage to motors and wiring when the current flowing through the electrical circuit supersedes its design limits. It does this by removing the current from a circuit when an unsafe condition arises

2.)

•NAME OF APPLIANCE/S SAFETY DEVICE•

"ELECTROCUTION PROTECTION"

•HOW IT FUNCTION•

Electrical shock occurs when the body becomes part of the electric circuit, either when an individual comes in contact with both wires of an electrical circuit, one wire of an energized circuit and the ground, or a metallic part that has become energized by contact with an electrical conductor

3.)

•NAME OF APPLIANCE/S SAFETY DEVICE•

"SURGE PROTECTION"

•HOW IT FUNCTION•

A surge protector guards against the damage that sudden power surges can cause. It works by pulling the current from one outlet and passing it through to the devices you have plugged into the surge protector

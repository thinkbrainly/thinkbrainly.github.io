5. 1. What is the difference between entrepreneurship and entrepreneur? 2. What are the entrepreneurial traits? Discuss the importance to the success of the business venture. 3. What is the relevance of entrepreneurship? Discuss how important it is in the country. ​


ANSWER:

1. The difference between entrepreneurship and entrepreneur is that entrepreneurship is the act of creating and running a business venture, while an entrepreneur is the person who starts and runs the business. An entrepreneur takes risks and is willing to invest their time and energy into the venture. They must have the skills and resources necessary to develop a business plan and then execute it. Entrepreneurship is often associated with innovation and creativity, while an entrepreneur is someone who can identify an opportunity and turn it into a profitable venture.

2. Entrepreneurial traits are the qualities that an individual needs to possess in order to be successful in a business venture. These traits include strong leadership skills, self-motivation, strong ethics and integrity, willingness to take risks, innovation, knowledge of the industry, financial literacy, and the ability to manage resources and people.

The importance of these traits to the success of a business venture cannot be overstated. Strong leadership skills are essential to motivate and inspire team members to work together to achieve a common goal. Self-motivation is necessary to stay focused and motivated to achieve success, while strong ethics and integrity are important to ensure that the business operates in an ethical and moral manner. Taking risks is essential to success, and innovation is key to staying ahead of the competition. Having a good understanding of the industry and financial literacy can help entrepreneurs make informed decisions that will be beneficial for the business, and being able to manage resources and people is essential for any business to run smoothly.

Overall, having the right traits and qualities is essential for entrepreneurs to succeed in business. Without them, it is unlikely that entrepreneurs will be able to achieve their desired goals and objectives.

3. Entrepreneurship is an important factor in driving economic growth and creating jobs in a country. Entrepreneurs create new businesses [1], taking on all the risks and rewards of the venture. This can bring about new products and services, create jobs, and stimulate economic activity. By taking risks and investing resources, entrepreneurs can also help to drive innovation and create new industries.

In addition, entrepreneurs can play an important role in promoting social and economic development by bringing about positive changes in their communities. This can include creating new businesses that provide employment opportunities, as well as providing access to essential services and resources that are not available in the area. This can help to reduce poverty and inequality, while also helping to build a more resilient and prosperous society.

The importance of entrepreneurship is further highlighted by the fact that it can provide individuals with a sense of purpose and fulfillment, as well as offering valuable skills and experiences that can be used in other areas of life. It can also help to foster creativity, drive innovation, and even provide an opportunity for entrepreneurs to make a positive change in the world. Ultimately, entrepreneurship is an essential part of any thriving economy, and its importance should not be underestimated.

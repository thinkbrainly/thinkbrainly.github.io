24. as a beginner artist, is it necessary to be trained in an art school/by a professional artist/art course in order to increase the chances of becoming a successful artist?​


You can become a professional artist without formal training, but it is less efficient, tends to take longer, and is much more difficult. Schooling in any subject area maximizes the opportunity to learn. Learning research indicates that people learn best and most efficiently with focused, goal oriented practice and feedback. In my high school art classes, sometimes a brief conversation with me will erase hours of difficulty that a student struggled through on their own.

Just drawing aimlessly is unlikely to result in more than minor and sporadic progress. I’ve answered questions here before from young artists who put in hour after hour with no results because they didn’t have focused, progressive goals just hours pushing a pencil around.

Good books are your best starting point, particularly those set up as Classroom-in-a-Book style. Work through all of the exercises, not just what interests you.

Practice diligently and then seek out knowledgeable feedback, which is the most difficult part. The opinions of other people who are in structured learning are helpful but not as much as a professional artist and not as much as someone who has studied how to help other people learn.

Look into taking classes of some kind even if they are just community classes to help get some feedback.

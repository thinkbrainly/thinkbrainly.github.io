5. Find the general solution of xy3dx+ex2dy=0


Answer:

ln(x) - e/(2y^2) = constant; in other words, y = 1 / (√(2ln(kx)) )

Step-by1-step explanation:

divide the expression xy^3dx+ex^2dy=0 by (x^2) x (y^3).

we get, dx/x + edy/y^3 = 0

Integrating , ∫(1/x)dx + ∫e(1/y^3)dy , we get

= > ln(x) + ey^(-2)/(-2) = constant

= >  ln(x) - e/(2y^2) = constant

= > e/(2y^2) = ln(x) - d       (where d is a constant)

Let d = ln(k)          (where k is a constant too)

= > e/(2y^2) = ln(x) - ln(k) = ln(x/k)

2y^2 = 1/ln(x/k)

y = 1 / (√(2ln(cx)) )      (where c = 1/k)

26. unbalanced stitches cause/remedies​


Causes

1. Incorrect tension of sewing thread.

2. Used incorrect thread path.

3. Incorrect adjustment of needle thread path.

4. Snagging of the needle with bobbin case and positioning finger.

5. If sewing threads are not lubricated.

Remedies

1. The setting of accurate tension to the thread.

2. Using of accurate thread path.

3. Using of right thread path.

4.Bobbin case to be smooth and finger positioning to be set again.

5. Better quality threads must be used here.

30. What do you think is the greatest reward for being ebedient?

Explanation:

hope it helps

Answer:

Rewards are not always monetary — they are the bonuses of life. They are given to adults or children for a job well done, whether it is on the playing field, good grades, or accomplishments on the job. Animals, too, are treated by their owner or trainer for listening to and obeying instructions.)

Rewards or favor from the Lord are often the results of our obedience to him. Whether we decide to obey depends on our love relationship with God and how we honor and prize his wisdom.

Jesus was an example of an obedient son who trusted his heavenly father. Proof of that is found when he prayed in the garden of Gethsemane prior to his betrayal, crucifixion and resurrection three days later. Jesus’ prayer is referenced in Luke 22:42: “Father, if it is your will, remove this cup from me; nevertheless, not my will but yours, be done.”

To me, this was an example of a wrenching obedience decision, but it yielded the reward of deliverance from sin and death for all generations.

Today, Christians may have the desire to be obedient, but it is similar to a tug-of-war game. On one end of the rope is our human nature as well as a propensity to just follow the behaviors of others. On the rope’s other end is the desire to respond positively to the authority of God, Jesus and the Holy Spirit. This obedience struggle — or rope tugging — happens frequently, especially in situations where we know the wise thing to do is to follow God’s instruction, but pleasing ourselves or others is a strong temptation to do the opposite.

Is disobedience learned or inherited? I think it’s both, not only from seeing my parents and friends slip up, but originally from the first parents, Adam and Eve. Their example of disobedience to God in the Garden of Eden resulted in separation from God’s presence. What separates us from God? Is it wanting our way and control, stubborn resistance, or unbelief? I believe it is all of the above.

While ignoring both God’s wisdom and biblical instructions has consequences, obedience to his commandments has rewards — even “great” rewards. Psalm 19:7-10 in the New Living Bible sets out the attributes of the Lord’s commands. In verse 11 it says, “They are a warning to those who hear them; there is great reward for those who obey them.”

One reward I take to heart is from Proverbs 3:1-2: “My son (or daughter) do not forget my law, but let your heart keep my commands; for length of days and long life and peace they will add to you.”

Long life with peace is ideal. In fact, not only peace, but “great peace” is stated in Psalm 119:165: “Those who love your law have great peace and do not stumble.”

These are verses to remember on days when fear, apprehension or anything else threatens our well-being.

God’s love was evident in his conversation with Moses after the delivery of the Ten Commandments to the people of Israel.

“Oh, that they (Israel) had such a heart in them that they would fear (reverence and worship) Me and always keep all my commandments, that it might be well with them and with their children forever!” (Deuteronomy 5:29).

Our obedience tug-of-war game will go on indefinitely until we pray for God’s power to follow him consistently, now and in the future. It may take a while to be consistent, but remember, the rewards are great.

13. What is your expectation on this subject NSTP


As a student, my expectation in  National Service Training Program (NSTP) subject is to know its purpose and become knowledgeable on its importance to Filipino youth. I believe that it will help me enhance my civic consciousness and leadership and develop my physical, moral, spiritual, and social well-being. Unlock a new set of skills that will surely help me both in my education and the path that I will take in the future. I expect to learn more about community involvement like participation in activities, volunteering, and I hope to know the basic preparedness for disaster, calamity, or emergency. Through this, I will also learn what my role is in society and the importance of society to humanity.

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) is developed for tertiary level students under Republic Act No. 9163. It is mandatory to take and complete at least one NSTP subject for students of any bachelor's degree or at least two-year technical-vocational courses in public and private educational institutions. The components of this program aim to enhance the youth's active contribution to the general welfare of society.

The NSTP consist of three (3) components:

Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) is optional and voluntary as this is difficult since it offers military training to college students. The Reserve Officers Training Corps (ROTC) is responsible for training and developing college students in the fundamentals of military service to generate capable reservists for the Philippine Armed Forces. The ROTC's specific goals are to (1) In the case of an emergency, prepare college students for prospective service in the defense establishment. (2) Be a reserved component for the Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) and (3) To train potential officers.The Literacy Training Service (LTS) is basically for Education students, but students of other departments that can teach literacy and numeracy can still choose it. This program aims to improve the prospective educator's knowledge, abilities, and beliefs. They can use it in teaching in the classroom. Also, they can volunteer to teach reading and numeracy to children of young age, out of school youth, indigenous children, and other segments of society that need this kind of service. The Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS) this component targets the general welfare of the community. The significance of this is for the students to know the importance of youth involvement that will benefit most of the community. Activities under this component include blood donation, basic first aid, disaster management, community-based program, skills training, environmental program, etc.

NSTP - National Service Training Program

Explanation:

NSTP is mandated by Phiippine law under Rebuplic Act 9163. National Servic Training Program is has 3 sections namely:

1. Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS)

This program component is designed to provide students with activities contributory to the general welfare and betterment of life of the members of the community especially those developed to improve social welfare services.

2. Literacy Training Service (LTS)

This program component is designed to train students in teaching literacy and numeracy skills to schoolchildren and out-of-school youths. The hope is to continue learning on a peer - to - peer interaction.

3. Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC)

This program component is designed to provide military education and training for students to mobilize them for national defense preparedness. This is also a glimpse for young people to see how military life is and encourage them into service.

10. jhun can finish an accounting work in 8 hours.Leo can finish the same work in 6 hours.After2 hours of working together,Jhun left Leo for lunch and leo finished the job.How l9ng does it take Leo to finish the job?


4 hours and 15 minutes

Let [tex]aw[/tex] stand for accounting work.

Jhun: [tex]\frac{1aw}{8hours}[/tex] ; Leo: [tex]\frac{1aw}{6hours}[/tex]

Convert both to similar fractions with LCM=24.

Jhun: [tex]\frac{3}{24}aw/hour[/tex] ; Leo: [tex]\frac{4}{24}aw/hour[/tex]

Now get their average working rate.

[tex]\frac{\frac{3}{24}+\frac{4}{24}}{2}=\frac{7}{24}[/tex] ÷ [tex]2[/tex]

change 2 to similar fraction with LCM=24 and cross multiply...

[tex]\frac{7}{24}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{24}{48}=\frac{168}{1152}=\frac{7}{48}[/tex]

Average: [tex]\frac{7}{48}aw/hour[/tex]

For 2 hours they worked together finishing...

[tex]\frac{7}{48}aw/hour[/tex] × [tex]2=\frac{14}{48}aw/2hours[/tex]

or [tex]\frac{7}{24}aw/2hours[/tex]

Now determine the part of accounting work that was left for Leo by subtracting the part they worked together from a whole similar fraction...

[tex]\frac{24}{24}-\frac{7}{24}=\frac{17}{24}aw[/tex]

Then divide it by Leo's working rate (cross multiply).

[tex]\frac{17}{24}aw[/tex] × [tex]\frac{24}{4}aw/hour=\frac{408}{96}hours[/tex]

[tex]=4.25hours[/tex]

Convert 0.25 hours to minutes.

[tex]0.25hour(\frac{60minutes}{hour})=15minutes[/tex]

Leo will take 4 hours and 15 minutes to finish the job.

26. Mrs. Cruz cooked their lunch at 10:35 a.m. If she finished cooking their lunch at 11:20 am., how long did their lunch? she cook! Analyze the problem and silve using the 4 stop plan​


Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: UNDERSTAND the problem - Mrs. Cruz cooked their lunch at 10:35 a.m. and finished cooking their lunch at 11:20 a.m.

Step 2: PLAN - To solve this problem, you need to find out the time difference between 10:35 a.m. and 11:20 a.m.

Step 3: SOLVE - The time difference between 10:35 a.m. and 11:20 a.m. is 45 minutes.

Step 4: LOOK BACK - Mrs. Cruz cooked their lunch for 45 minutes.

Answer:

Analysis:

Mrs. Cruz cooked their lunch at 10:35 a.m.

She finished cooking their lunch at 11:20 a.m.

We need to find out how long did she cook their lunch.

Plan:

Identify what is known and what needs to be found out.

Known: start time (10:35 a.m.), end time (11:20 a.m.)

Unknown: how long did she cook their lunch

Choose a formula or create one if needed.

To find out the duration, we can subtract the start time from the end time.

Substitute the values.

Duration = end time - start time

Duration = 11:20 a.m. - 10:35 a.m.

Solve the problem.

Subtract the minutes: 20 - 35 = -15

Since the difference is negative, we need to borrow an hour from the hour column.

Add 60 minutes to 20: 20 + 60 = 80

Subtract 35 from 80: 80 - 35 = 45

Answer: Mrs. Cruz cooked their lunch for 45 minutes.

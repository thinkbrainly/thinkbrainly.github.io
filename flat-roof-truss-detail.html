3. enumerate the different components of roof truss and their function.​


Answer:

Apex – Highest point where the sloping top chords meet.Bearing – Structural support of trusses (usually walls) normally with a timber wall plate.Bottom Chords (BC) – the lowest longitudinal member of a truss.Cantilever – part of structural member that extends beyond its support.Cantilever Strut – web that joins the bottom chord above the bearing point to the top chord of a cantilevered truss.Chord – main members that form the outline of the truss and subject to relatively large axial forces and bending moments.Clear Span – horizontal distance between interior edges of supports.Heel – point on a truss which top and bottom chord intersect.Nail Plate – galvanized steel plate punched to form a nail pattern integral with the plate and used to connect timber members.Node (node point) – point of intersection of two or more members that make up the panels of a truss.Nominal Span – horizontal distance between outside edges of the supports (wall plates) – usually the tie beam length.Joint – point of intersection of one or more web(s) with a chord.

Answer:

Yes, galvanized trusses are a great choice for roofing fasteners because they are highly resistant to corrosion. This is especially important when building a house because of the exposure to the elements. Galvanized trusses are coated with a layer of zinc, which helps to protect the underlying steel from corrosion due to exposure to water, salt, and other corrosive materials. This layer of zinc helps to slow down the corrosion process, giving the trusses a longer life. Additionally, galvanized trusses instill trust because they are a proven, reliable method for roofing fasteners.

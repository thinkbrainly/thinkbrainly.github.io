2. Determine the type of bond ( Ionic, polar covalent or non-polar covalent) that will from between the following atoms.( show the LEDS)CBr⁴​


Answer:

The bond between Carbon (C) and Bromine (Br) in CBr4 is a polar covalent bond. The bond is covalent because it involves the sharing of electrons between the atoms, but it is polar because there is a difference in electronegativity between the two elements. The bond will be polar covalent because Carbon (C) has an electronegativity of 2.55 and Bromine (Br) has an electronegativity of 2.96. The difference in electronegativity causes the electrons to be shared unequally, resulting in a polar bond.

21. One of the components of photocells and space vehicles is rubidium. How many moles are there in 9.50 g of Rb? The molar mass of Rb is 85.47 g/mol.​


[tex]\large\underline{\rm{PROBLEM}:}[/tex]

One of the components of photocells and space vehicles is rubidium. How many moles are there in 9.50 g of Rb? The molar mass of Rb is 85.47 g/mol.

[tex]\tt\color{black}{\overline{\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\underline{\rm{ANSWER}:}[/tex]

[tex]\: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \:[/tex] ↳ [tex]\large{{\tt\color{skyblue}{0.111 \: moles \: of \: Rb}}}[/tex]

[tex]\tt\color{black}{\overline{\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\underline{\rm{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

⇛To find the number of moles in 9.50 g of Rb, we can use the formula: [tex]\small{{\tt{moles(n) \: = \: \frac{mass(m)}{molar \: mass(m)} \\ }}}[/tex]

[tex]\small{{\tt{moles(n) \: = \: \frac{mass(m)}{molar \: mass(m)} \\ }}}[/tex]

[tex]\small{{\tt{moles(n) \: = \: \frac{9.50 \: g}{85.47 \: g/mol} \\ }}}[/tex]

[tex]\small{{\tt\color{skyblue}{moles(n) \: = \: 0.111}}}[/tex]

∵　Therefore, there are 0.111 moles of rubidium in 9.50 g of Rb.

7. The power loss in a transmission line is proportional to the square of the current. When the current carried by a transmission line is doubled (or from 1A to 2A), the power loss in the by a factor or transmission line is​


Answer:

When the current carried by a transmission line is doubled (or from 1A to 2A), the power loss in the transmission line is increased by a factor of four (4).

This can be shown using the formula for power loss in a transmission line:

P = I^2 * R

where P is the power loss, I is the current, and R is the resistance of the transmission line.

If the current is doubled to 2A, the power loss becomes:

P = (2A)^2 * R = 4A^2 * R

This means that the power loss has increased by a factor of 4, since 4A^2 is four times greater than 1A^2. Therefore, the power loss in the transmission line increases exponentially as the current increases.

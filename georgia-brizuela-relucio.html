Country Overview

Explanation:

1.1.1.WATER RESOURCES Georgia can be divided into two main river basin groups: -The Black Sea basin, in the west of the country. The internal renewable surface water resources (IRSWR) generated in this basin are estimated at 42.5km3/year. The main rivers are, from north to south, the Inguri, the Rioni and the Chorokhi. The main stream of the Chorokhi rises in Turkey (the Corub River) and the estimated inflow from Turkey is 6.3km3/year. -The Caspian Sea basin, in the east of the country. The IRSWR generated in this basin are estimated at 14.4km3/year. The main rivers are, from north to south: the Terek and the Andiyskoye, which rise in the north of the country and flow northeast to the Russian Federation before entering the Caspian Sea; the Alazani, the Lori and the Kura, which rise in Georgia and flow into Azerbaijan in Lake Adzhinour, before flowing southeast in Azerbaijan and then entering the Caspian Sea.

3. Write your realization about Armenia & Georgia? Best Things to do in Armenia & Georgia.​


Answer:

Armenia and Georgia are two fascinating countries in the South Caucasus region with rich cultural and historical heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious cuisine. Here are some of the best things to do in each country:

Armenia:

- Visit the ancient city of Yerevan, the capital city of Armenia, and explore its old town, museums, and galleries.

- Take a trip to the majestic Mount Ararat, which is considered a sacred symbol of Armenia and is visible from many parts of the country.

- Visit the ancient Monastery of Geghard, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which is carved into a mountain and houses some of the country's most precious religious artifacts.

- Explore the ruins of the ancient city of Ani, located on the border between Armenia and Turkey, and marvel at its impressive architecture and historical significance.

- Sample the delicious Armenian cuisine, which includes dishes such as khorovats (barbecue meat), dolma (stuffed grape leaves), and lavash (traditional Armenian bread).

Georgia:

- Visit the charming capital city of Tbilisi and explore its historic old town, churches, and museums.

- Take a trip to the stunning Caucasus Mountains, which offer breathtaking views and opportunities for hiking, skiing, and other outdoor activities.

- Visit the ancient cave city of Uplistsikhe, which dates back to the 6th century BC and is one of the oldest settlements in the region.

- Explore the charming wine region of Kakheti, which is known for its delicious wines, beautiful vineyards, and historic sites.

- Sample the delicious Georgian cuisine, which includes dishes such as khinkali (dumplings), khachapuri (cheese-filled bread), and shashlik (barbecue meat).

Overall, Armenia and Georgia offer a wealth of cultural, historical, and natural attractions that are sure to delight any traveler. Whether you're interested in exploring ancient ruins, sampling delicious cuisine, or simply enjoying the stunning scenery, these countries have something to offer for everyone.

Describes diversity of Georgia's vegetation

The book describes richness and diversity of Georgia’s vegetation. Contrasting ecosystems coexist on the relatively small territory of the country and include semi-deserts in East Georgia, Colchic forests with almost sub-tropical climate in West Georgia and subnival plant communities in high mountains. West Georgia lacks xerophilous vegetation zone and mesophilous forest vegetation spreads from the sea level to subalpine zone. The Colchic refugium (West Georgia) ensured survival of the Tertiary’s mesophilous forest flora. Vertical profile of the vegetation is more complex in East Georgia with semi-desert, steppe and arid open forest zone. 18. pagpapahalagang kultural ng Georgia​


Answer:

Ang Georgia ay isang mayaman sa kultura at magkakaibang estado na nag-aalok ng isang bagay para sa lahat. Mula sa mataong buhay sa lungsod ng Atlanta hanggang sa nakamamanghang tanawin ng kabundukan ng North Georgia, talagang mayroong bagay para sa lahat sa Georgia.

Ang estado ay tahanan ng iba't ibang kultura, kabilang ang African American, Caucasian, Hispanic, at Native American. Maaaring maranasan ng mga bisita ang kakaibang kultura ng bawat grupo sa pamamagitan ng pagbisita sa iba't ibang atraksyon sa buong estado.

Ang ilan sa mga dapat makitang kultural na atraksyon sa Georgia ay kinabibilangan ng Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site sa Atlanta, na ginugunita ang buhay at gawain ng isa sa pinakamahalagang pinuno ng karapatang sibil sa kasaysayan. Maaari ding libutin ng mga bisita ang gusali ng Georgia State Capitol, na naglalaman ng maraming makasaysayang artifact mula sa mahaba at mayamang kasaysayan ng estado.

Para sa mga interesadong maranasan ang magkakaibang kultura ng pagkain ng estado, mayroong isang bilang ng mga mahuhusay na restaurant sa buong Georgia na nag-aalok ng iba't ibang mga lutuin mula sa buong mundo. At hindi kumpleto ang pagbisita sa Georgia nang hindi nakakatikim ng ilan sa mga masasarap na lokal na peach pie at iba pang katakam-takam na dessert!

20. who is GEORGIA TOTTO O'KEEFFE? ​


Answer:

Georgia Totto O'Keeffe (November 15, 1887 – March 6, 1986) was an American artist. She was known for her paintings of enlarged flowers, New York skyscrapers, and New Mexico landscapes. O'Keeffe has been recognized as the "Mother of American modernism".

Answer:

Georgia Totto O'Keeffe (November 15, 1887 – March 6, 1986) was an American artist. She was known for her paintings of enlarged flowers, New York skyscrapers, and New Mexico landscapes. O'Keeffe has been recognized as the "Mother of American modernism". In 1905, O'Keeffe began her serious formal art training at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago[3]and then the Art Students League of New York, but she felt constrained by her lessons that emphasised the recreation or copying of nature. In 1908, unable to fund further education, she worked for two years as a commercial illustrator and then taught in Virginia, Texas, and South Carolina between 1911 and 1918. During that time, she studied art during the summers between 1912 and 1914 and was introduced to the principles and philosophies of Arthur Wesley Dow, who created works of art based upon personal style, design, and interpretation of subjects, rather than trying to copy or represent them. This caused a major change in the way she felt about and approached art, as seen in the beginning stages of her watercolors from her studies at the University of Virginia and more dramatically in the charcoal drawings that she produced in 1915 that led to total abstraction. Alfred Stieglitz, an art dealer and photographer, held an exhibit of her works in 1917.[4] Over the next couple of years, she taught and continued her studies at the Teachers College, Columbia University in 1914 and 1915.

22. pambansang kasuotan sa Georgia


Ang bansang Georgia ay kilala mayaman nilang kultura pagdating sa iba’t ibang paniniwala kagaya ng relihiyon, wika, musika at sayaw at maging sa iba’t ibang estilo ng pananamit. Kagaya ng ibang bansa, ang pambansang kasuotan ng Georgia ay parte na ng mayaman at masaganang tradisyon ng bansang Georgia.

Isang orihinal na sining ang pambansang kasuotan ng Georgia. Ito ay isang mahaba at masikip na uri ng isang damit na kung tawagi ay “kartuli”. Karaniwan itong pinaparisan ng mahabang palda at simpleng piraso ng alahas. Kung pagbabasihan ang ekonomiya ng Georgia masasabi natin na ang kanilang kasuotan ay hindi lamang salamin ng kultura at tradisyon ng Georgia. Ito ay salamin rin ng kanilang estado sa buhay.

Karaniwan na ring maituturing sa estilo ng pananamit ng mga Georgian ang pagpapares ng iba’t ibang dekorasyon at sombrelo sa kanilang pambansang kasuotan.

Answer:

(please ask me if you got any questions about my answer thx.)

The transport strike in the Philippines was caused by the government's plan to phase out jeepneys and other old public utility vehicles (PUVs) and replace them with modern, eco-friendly vehicles. This plan has been met with resistance from jeepney drivers and operators who fear losing their livelihoods.

The transport strike was called by various transport groups and labor organizations, including the Pinagkaisang Samahan ng mga Tsuper at Operator Nationwide (PISTON) and the Kilusang Mayo Uno (KMU).

The transport strike affected different types of public transport, including jeepneys, buses, UV Express vans, and some taxi services. Many of these vehicles remained parked in protest, causing significant disruption to the daily commute of millions of Filipinos.

Commuters coped with the strike by taking alternative modes of transportation, such as the MRT/LRT trains, tricycles, and motorbikes. However, these alternative modes of transport were often overcrowded and insufficient to meet the demand, leading to longer waiting times and higher fares.

The transport strike had a significant impact on the economy, society, and the environment. The disruption to public transport caused by the strike resulted in losses for businesses and lowered productivity. The strike also highlighted the challenges facing the transport sector and brought attention to the need for more sustainable and efficient modes of transportation.

The strike also had social impacts, with commuters experiencing inconvenience, stress, and uncertainty. In terms of the environment, the strike highlighted the need to address the issue of air pollution caused by old and outdated PUVs.

23. Similarities of revers and strike slipand similarities of revers,normal,strike slipand similarities of normal and strike slip​


Answer:

Normal Faults and Reverse Faults are "Dip-Slip" Faults - they experience vertical movement, in line with the dip of the fault. They are identified by the relative movement of the Hanging Wall and Foot Wall.

In a Normal Fault, the hanging wall moves downwards relative to the foot wall. They are caused by extensional tectonics. This kind of faulting will cause the faulted section of rock to lengthen.

In a Reverse Fault, the hanging wall moves upwards relative to the foot wall. They are caused by compressional tectonics. This kind of faulting will cause the faulted section of rock to shorten.

A Strike-Slip fault experiences lateral motion - movement is horizontal, along the line of the strike of the fault. They can be defined as Sinistral (the walls move to the left relative to one another) or Dextral (the walls move to the right relative to one another).

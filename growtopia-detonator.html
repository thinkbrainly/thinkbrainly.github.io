This reaction releases energy in the form of heat and light, which can cause the material to ignite or even detonate if the temperature is high enough.

Answer:There are several types of combustible solids that can ignite or detonate at lower temperatures than normal materials. These include:

Pyrophoric materials: These are substances that ignite spontaneously in air at or below room temperature. Examples include finely divided metals such as iron, magnesium, and titanium.

Organic peroxides: These are highly reactive chemicals that can undergo self-accelerating decomposition and explosive reactions. They are often used as initiators or catalysts in polymerization reactions, but they can also be found in some household items such as hair bleach.

Nitrocellulose: This is a highly flammable material that is often used in the manufacture of explosives and propellants. It is also used in some consumer products, such as nail polish remover.

Aluminum powder: This is a highly reactive material that can ignite or explode when exposed to heat, friction, or shock. It is often used in pyrotechnics, but can also be found in some industrial applications.

Silane: This is a colorless gas that can ignite spontaneously in air or on contact with water. It is often used in the semiconductor industry, but can also be found in some specialty chemicals.

It is important to handle these materials with care and follow all safety protocols to avoid accidents or explosions.

24. what if you detonate bomb in volcano


The bomb would melt without starting a nuclear reaction.

A nuclear bomb is made of explosives and a radioactive core (for example, plutonium). To ignite a devastating explosion, the explosives all need to detonate under careful timing to create enough energy to start a nuclear reaction in the core. Once the chain reaction starts, there's no stopping it. However, if a nuclear bomb was plonked into a chamber of magma, it would melt – shell, radioactive core and all.

In order to start this essential reaction, and create a catastrophic explosion, the bomb would need to be detonated above the volcano mouth, or magically transported into the magma chamber's center.

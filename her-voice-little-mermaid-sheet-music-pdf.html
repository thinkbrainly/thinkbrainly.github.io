13. what can be inferred about the author's intention in creating The little Mermaid ​


Answer:

Explanation:

In the case of "The Little Mermaid," it can be inferred that the author may have intended to teach young readers the importance of self-sacrifice and the consequences of making deals with powerful forces. The story follows the mermaid's journey as she gives up her voice and endures physical pain in order to gain a human soul and the love of a prince, ultimately realizing the price she paid was too high.

It is also possible that the author intended to use "The Little Mermaid" as a commentary on societal norms and gender roles during the time it was written. The mermaid's desire to become human and win the love of a prince could be interpreted as a critique of the societal pressure placed on women to conform to traditional gender roles and marry for status and wealth rather than true love. However, these interpretations are subjective and may vary depending on the reader's perspective.

29. what do you think is the theme of the little mermaid story? Explain ​


Explanation:

The theme of "The Little Mermaid" story is the pursuit of true and love self-sacrifice. The story follows the journey of a young mermaid who falls in love with a human prince and is willing to give up everything she knows to be with him. She trades her beautiful voice and endures immense pain to gain a chance to be with him. The story shows that true love is not just about physical attraction or superficial qualities, but about selfless sacrifice and the willingness to give up everything for the person you love. At the end of the story, the little mermaid chooses to sacrifice her own life to save the prince, which shows the ultimate act of love and selflessness. The story also highlights the importance of making choices and accepting the consequences that come with them. Additionally, it touches upon the theme of transformation, as the little mermaid transforms from a sea creature to a human.

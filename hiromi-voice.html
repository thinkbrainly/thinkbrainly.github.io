5. when do we say that the verb is in the active voice?passive vioice?


Answer:

In most English sentences with an action verb, the subject performs the action denoted by the verb. These examples show that the subject is doing the verb's action. Because the subject does or "acts upon" the verb in such sentences, the sentences are said to be in the active voice.

Active voice means that a sentence has a subject that acts upon its verb. Passive voice means that a subject is a recipient of a verb's action.

10. _____ allows people to make voice calls through the Internet. a. VoIP b. Online Chat c. Videoconferencing d. Wiki


Letter A

VoIP or Voice Over IP is the one that allows people to make voice calls through the internet. It is the transmission of voice and other contents like multimedia (videos and pictures) over IP or internet protocol networks. Some VoIP allows people to call others using the same technology (VoIP) while others allow people to call someone who is using a telephone number, whether it is mobile, long distance or even long distance number.

VoIP serves as a converter in the network. In order for the information to be transmitted, VoIP changes the data into digital data. The use of another device is needed to bring back the information to its standard form (e.i., voice which is an analog data).

20. What is the passive voice of the noice outside disturbed the students


Answer:

Some students think that using the passive voice makes them sound more academic, but (as in the above example) when it is misused, it just sounds wordy.

Answer:

The passive voice is used to emphasize the person or object that experiences an action (in this case, “you” being “disturbed”) rather than the person or object that performs the action (in this case “the noise” that does the “disturbing”). In passive voice, the person or object experiencing the action is connected with the “TO BE” form of the verb (in this case, “You ARE disturbed…” or in question format, “ARE you disturbed…?”)

Interestingly, some people would think that “you” must be the subject (since it is a person), which would make “the noise” the object—and therefore “The noise disturbs you” (or “Does the noise disturb you?”) might be considered the passive voice. But it is actually active (note that it does not include the “to be” verb).

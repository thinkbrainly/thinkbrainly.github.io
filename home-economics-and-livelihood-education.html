14. Subject: Home Economics and Livelihood Education (HELE) Give three disadvantages for each category of plant propagation.


Answer:

Seed Propagation:

1. Seeds can be expensive.

2. Seeds may not always produce true-to-type plants.

3. Seeds may take a long time to germinate.

Cuttings Propagation:

1. Cuttings can be difficult to establish and require more skill to do successfully.

2. Cuttings can be more prone to disease and pests than seeds.

3. Cuttings require more resources and tools than other methods of propagation.

Layering Propagation:

1. Layering can take a long time to produce a mature plant.

2. Layering requires a certain amount of skill to be successful.

3. Layering requires more space than other methods of propagation.

5. how to make a feasibility study​


A feasibility study is an important step in starting a new business. It is a research and analysis of a business idea to determine if it is viable. The study looks at the economic, technical, and legal feasibility of the business idea.

There are several steps to conducting a feasibility study. The first step is to research the business idea. This includes looking at the industry, the target market, and the competition. The second step is to analyze the financial viability of the business idea. This includes looking at the start-up costs, the operating costs, and the revenue potential. The third step is to assess the technical feasibility of the business idea. This includes looking at the production process, the technology requirements, and the skills needed to execute the business idea. The fourth step is to evaluate the legal feasibility of the business idea. This includes looking at the business structure, the licenses and permits required, and the compliance with regulations.

After conducting a feasibility study, the entrepreneur can decide if the business idea is viable and worth pursuing. If the feasibility study is positive, the next step is to develop a business plan. If the feasibility study is negative, the entrepreneur may decide to modify the business idea or pursue a different business altogether.

19. how do you prepare a feasibility study?​


Answer:

1. Conduct a Preliminary Analysis

The primary purpose of the preliminary analysis is to screen project ideas before extensive time, effort, and money are invested. Two sets of activities are involved.

2.Prepare a Projected Income Statement

Anticipated income must cover direct and indirect costs, taking into account the expected income growth curve. Working backward from the anticipated income, the revenue necessary to generate that income can be derived in order to build a projected income statement.

3.Conduct a Market Survey

A good market survey is crucial. If the planner cannot perform this survey, an outside firm should be hired. The primary objective of a market survey is a realistic projection of revenues. The major steps include:

4.Plan Business Organization and Operations

At this point, the organization and operations of the business should be planned in sufficient depth to determine the technical feasibility and costs involved in start-up, fixed investment, and operations.

5.Prepare an Opening Day Balance Sheet

The Opening Day Balance Sheet should reflect the practice's assets and liabilities as accurately as possible at the time the practice begins, before the practice generates income.

6.Review and Analyze All Data

This review is crucial. The planner should determine if any data or analysis performed should change any of the preceding analyses. Basically, taking this step means "Step back and reflect one more time."

7.Make "Go/No Go" Decision

All the preceding steps have been aimed at providing data and analysis for the "go/no go" decision. If the analysis indicates that the business should yield at least the desired minimum income and has growth potential, a "go" decision is appropriate. Anything less mandates a "no go" decision.

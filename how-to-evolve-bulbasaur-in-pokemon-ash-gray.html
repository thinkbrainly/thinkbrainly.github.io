1. explain how does management evolve and how they evolve the aspect?​


Explanation:

Management is the process of leading and directing a group or organization to achieve goals. It evolves by adapting to changes in technology, workforce, and organizational structure. The most prominent aspect that evolves is the management style, which adapts to the current situation.

Explanation:

Management has evolved over time as a way to organize and coordinate resources to achieve goals and objectives. The evolution of management can be broken down into several key stages:

The Classical Management era: This period, which lasted from the late 1800s to the early 1900s, saw the development of the scientific management approach by Frederick Taylor and Henry Fayol. This approach focused on increasing efficiency and productivity through the use of time and motion studies, standardization, and the separation of planning and execution.

The Behavioral Management era: During the 1920s and 1930s, a new approach to management emerged, known as the behavioral approach. This approach focused on the human aspects of management, such as motivation, communication, and leadership, and was developed by researchers such as Elton Mayo and Abraham Maslow.

The Quantitative Management era: In the 1950s and 1960s, management science and operations research techniques, such as mathematical modeling and decision-making tools, were applied to management problems. This era saw the development of quantitative management techniques such as linear programming and decision theory.

The Contingency Management era: In the 1970s and 1980s, the contingency approach to management emerged, which emphasized the need for management to adapt to different situations. This approach recognized that different problems and situations require different management approaches, and that there is no one best way to manage.

The Systematic Management era: In the 1990s and 2000s, management started to adopt a more holistic and systemic approach. This era saw the development of the Total Quality Management (TQM) approach, which emphasized the need for continuous improvement and the participation of all employees in the management process.

Nowadays, management is in a constant evolution, with new technologies, new management styles, and new ways of thinking, it also includes the aspect of sustainability, digitalization, and adaptability to changes in the environment

16. How Taxation evolved ?


Taxation has a long history, dating back to ancient civilizations. In ancient Egypt, for example, the pharaohs imposed taxes on various commodities, such as grain and livestock, to fund public works projects and other expenses. Similarly, the Roman Empire used taxation to fund its military and other governmental activities.

Throughout history, taxation has played a critical role in the funding of governments and the provision of public goods and services. In modern times, taxation has evolved to become more complex, with different countries adopting different systems for collecting taxes and using the revenue generated in various ways.

In the United States, for example, the federal government uses tax revenue to fund a wide range of programs and services, including defense, education, healthcare, and social welfare. The U.S. tax system is progressive, meaning that those who earn more pay a higher percentage of their income in taxes.

Other countries have different systems of taxation, such as a flat tax or a value-added tax (VAT), which is a tax on the value added to a product at each stage of production. Some countries also have a mix of different tax systems, with different types of taxes applied to different types of income or transactions.

Overall, the evolution of taxation has been shaped by economic, political, and social factors, and it is likely to continue to evolve in response to these and other influences in the future.

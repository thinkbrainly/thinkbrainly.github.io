1. Why is germinating calamansi seed important in producing calamansi seedlings?


Answer:

Like most other citrus trees, calamansi grows readily from seed. This is a reliable propagation method if you cannot find calamansi saplings or cuttings at a plant nursery.

Calamansi, also known as Philippine lime, is a small citrus fruit that is commonly used for cooking, seasoning, and beverage flavoring. It is rich in Vitamin C, antioxidants, and other essential nutrients which make it a valuable addition to any dish or drink.

Planting calamansi seeds is a simple process that can be done both indoors and outdoors. Here are the steps to follow:

First, choose fresh and healthy seeds which you can get from a mature fruit. Remove the seeds from the fruit and wash them thoroughly to remove any flesh or pulp residues. Dry them with a clean cloth or paper towel.

Next, fill a small pot or container with a good quality potting mix or soil. Make a small hole in the center of the soil with your finger or a pencil.

Place one or two seeds in the hole and cover them with soil, making sure that they are not too deep (about ½ inch deep). Firm the soil gently around the seeds.

Water the soil thoroughly but gently with a watering can or spray bottle. Avoid over-watering as it can lead to soggy or waterlogged soil which can harm the seeds.

Cover the pot or container with a clear plastic bag or cling wrap to create a humid environment for the seeds. Place it in a warm and bright location, but avoid direct sunlight.

Check the soil regularly and water it when it feels dry to the touch. Be patient as it may take 2-3 weeks or more for the seeds to germinate.

Once the seedlings have sprouted and developed a few pairs of leaves, remove the plastic cover and transplant them into individual pots or outdoor soil. Give them enough light, water, and nutrients to grow healthy and strong.

In conclusion, planting and germinating calamansi seeds is a fun and rewarding activity that can provide you with fresh and flavorful fruits for years to come. With these simple steps and some TLC, you can enjoy the benefits of a citrus tree right in your own backyard or kitchen. Thank you

4. how important is the oxygen for the germinating seed?


How important is the oxygen for the germinating seed?

 

Oxygen is required by the germinating seed for metabolism.

 

Oxygen is being used for aerobic respiration, which is the main source of the energy of the seedling until the leaves grow.

 

Oxygen is an atmospheric gas that can be found within the soil pore spaces.

 

If the seed is buried too deep or waterlogged, the seed can be starved from oxygen.

It’s happened again. Despite trying to make sure I used up all my purchased seeds a few packets have somehow escaped my notice and have now passed their use-by date. I could simply throw them away and buy new ones, but I don’t like spending money if I can help it! However, if I sow them and they don’t germinate I will be wasting potting mix and my time. So instead, it’s time for a simple germination test.

Why Test Seed Viability?

Testing seed viability is worthwhile when, like me, you want to take a punt on old seeds that are past their use-by date, or if you accidentally tore off the use-by date on the seed packet and have no idea how long that seed packet has been kicking around your seed box (yep – done that too!).

There’s no need to test seeds that are within their use-by date, unless they’ve been incorrectly stored. Seeds stored at too high a temperature or in humid conditions may turn out to be duds, but a germination test will let you know for sure.

Self-saved seeds can also benefit from a viability test if they’re approaching the end of their typical shelf life, or if it’s your first try at saving seeds from a new plant or variety. This way you can be sure that your technique is sound and your storage conditions optimal.

The Philippine Eagle is one of the world's rarest eagles. It's a massive bird of prey that can only be found on four Philippine islands: Luzon, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao.

The name "Monkey-eating Eagle" was given to it by mistake. Philippine Eagles hunt on a variety of species, both large and little, in addition to monkeys. Bats, civets, flying squirrels, and macaques have all been reported as prey. They also consume other birds of prey, as well as snakes and lizards. A Philippine Eagle's diet varies based on where it lives on the island.

very year, at least one Philippine Eagle is killed as a result of shooting. As more of our forest is gone, Philippine Eagles are forced to travel further and further away from their typical hunting areas in search of prey. This generally leads them near human settlements and cattle, resulting in combat, with the Philippine Eagle coming out on the losing end.

The Philippine Eagle, are being the top of the food chain species, plays a critical role in maintaining a ecosystem's delicate equilibrium. It aids in the natural regulation of the species populations and also provides a protective cover for all other living forms in its domain.

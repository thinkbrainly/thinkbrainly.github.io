Psychological Alienation When a person withdraws or isolates themselves from their surroundings or other people, it is known as alienation. People who exhibit signs of alienation frequently turn away from friends, family, and society. Additionally, they could exhibit sentiments of separation and estrangement, even from their own emotions.

Teaching of each from the Genesis story:

Genesis then traces the history of some of our oldest ancestors after detailing creation and the introduction of sin into the earth.

Some of them provide us with illustrations of sound spiritual, physical, and psychological concepts, while others highlight the issues that humans face as a result of our sinful nature.

For instance, once each made a rebellious decision, Adam and Eve instantly placed the blame elsewhere (Genesis 3:12-13)

Sociological AlienationSocial alienation is the sensation of being cut off from a group to whom one feels a connection, such as friends, family, or the larger community.

Teaching of each from the Genesis story:

But Cain's attitude was not the subject of God's answer to his rage. Instead, God commanded Cain to acknowledge his anger and then act morally in the face of it (Genesis 4:6-7).

Ecological AlienationThe natural world as it is defined as a directly visible environment with its wildness, landscape, resources, wild life, etc., is disconnected from or separated from by humans.

Man was created "in the image of God," according to Genesis 1:26. Although it doesn't go into detail, we can start to understand what it means to be created in God's image by getting to know Him.

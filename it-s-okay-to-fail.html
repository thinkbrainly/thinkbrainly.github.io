Answer:

Secondary waves (S-waves) are shear waves that are transverse in nature. ... S-waves are slower than P-waves, and speeds are typically around 60% of that of P-waves in any given material. Shear waves can't travel through any liquid medium, so the absence of S-waves in earth's outer core suggests a liquid state.

8. SamSon Electronics Company would like to come up with an LED TV such that its screen is 560 square inches larger than the present ones. Suppose the length of the screen of the larger TV is 6 inches longer than its width and the area of the smaller TV is 520 square inches. What is the length of the screen of the larger LED TV? a. 26 in. b. 30 in c. 33 in. d. 36 in


Answer:

The length of the screen of the larger LED TV is letter d. 36 in.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us find first the area of the screen of the larger LED TV.

Since the screen of the larger LED TV is 560 square inches larger than the present ones and the area of the screen of the smaller LED TV is 520 square inches, then we have to add 560 square inches and 520 square inches to get the area of the screen of the larger LED TV.

That is, 560 + 520 = 1080 square inches, the area of the screen of the larger LED TV.

Let x be the width of the screen of the larger LED TV

x + 6 be the length of the screen of the larger LED TV

1080 square inches be the area of the screen of the larger LED TV

The formula in finding the area of the rectangle is A = l(w).

A =(l)(w)

1080 = (x + 6)(x)     Substituting in the formula

1080 = x^2 + 6x     Applying Distributive Property of Multiplication over Addition

1080 + (-1080) = x^2 + 6x + (-1080)  Applying Addition Property of Equality (APE)

0 = x^2 + 6x - 1080

x^2 + 6x - 1080 = 0     Quadratic Equation

(x + 36) (x - 30) = 0      Applying factoring

x + 36 = 0       Equating the first factor to zero.

x + 36 + (-36) = 0 + (-36)   Applying Addition Property of Equality (APE)

x = -36 There is no negative value for width.

x - 30 = 0    Equating the second factor to zero.

x - 30 + 30 = 0 + 30  Applying Addition Property of Equality (APE)

x = 30 inches, the width of the screen of the larger LED TV

Substitute the value of x to find the length of the screen of the larger LED TV,

length = x + 6

= 30 + 6

=36 inches, the length of the screen of the larger LED TV

The voltage across the elements in a circuit is given by

[tex]\boxed{V = IR}[/tex]

where:

I = current

V = voltage

R = resistance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the voltage

V = IR

V = ([tex]2 \times 10^{-3}[/tex] A)(900 ohm)

[tex]\boxed{V = \text{1.8 V}}[/tex]

Therefore, the voltage output of the adaptor is 1.8 V.

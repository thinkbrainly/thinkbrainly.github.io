3. What is a letter of intent?


ANSWER:

A letter of intent for promotion is a letter that is written by an employee requesting promotion. Such a letter must reflect the employee's value and contributions to the company and his potential to excel when given greater responsibility. The letter should be objective and should state the employee's number of years at the company, positions held and awards won while on the job.

12. letter of intent applying a new job​


Answer:

A letter of intent, also known as a cover letter, is a document that you can include with your job application to provide additional information about your qualifications, experience, and interest in the position you are applying for. Here are some tips for writing a letter of intent for a new job:

1.Start with a strong introduction that explains who you are and why you are interested in the job.

2.Mention any relevant experience or qualifications that make you a good fit for the position.

3.Explain why you are interested in working for this particular company.

4.Use specific examples to illustrate your skills and achievements.

5.Keep the tone of your letter professional and avoid using overly casual language.

6.Close the letter by thanking the employer for considering your application and expressing your hope to be invited for an interview.

7.Edit and proofread your letter carefully to ensure that it is error-free.

I hope these tips help you write a strong letter of intent for your new job

17. What type of educational assistance is available to PWDs?​


Answer:

PWDs (Persons with Disabilities) are entitled to various educational assistance to help them pursue their academic goals. Here are some types of educational assistance available to PWDs:

Inclusive education: Inclusive education is an educational approach that aims to provide equal opportunities to PWDs by integrating them into mainstream schools. This approach ensures that PWDs receive the same quality education as their non-disabled peers.

Assistive technology: Assistive technology includes tools, devices, and software that help PWDs with mobility, communication, and learning. Examples include hearing aids, screen readers, and voice recognition software.

Special education: Special education is a type of education designed to meet the specific needs of PWDs. This includes personalized instruction, accommodations, and modifications to the curriculum.

Scholarships: Scholarships are financial aid provided to PWDs to help them pursue their academic goals. Many organizations and foundations offer scholarships specifically for PWDs.

Grants: Grants are funds provided by the government or private organizations to support educational programs for PWDs. These grants can be used to fund research, develop new educational programs, and provide resources for PWDs.

Disability support services: Disability support services are provided by colleges and universities to help PWDs with academic and non-academic activities. Examples include note-taking assistance, extended test time, and accessible classrooms.

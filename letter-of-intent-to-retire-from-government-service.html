2. When a priest retires from active service, he ceases to be a priest?


Answer:

When a priest retires from active duty, he retains his priesthood. As the saying goes, "once a priest, always a priest." Priests can leave their "jobs" as pastors, chancery officers, chaplains, and other positions. Of course, you're always a priest. A retired priest has more latitude in accepting supply requests. For example, he could show up on Sunday and say mass, giving the pastor the day off, or he could travel, act as a chaplain on a tour or cruise, and research whatever he wants. They could serve as mentors to younger priests.

21. A company determines the age at which an employee can retire with full pension fromthe formula A+B=90 where a is the employee's age and B is the number ofyears of service.Directions: Find the years of service for retirement with full pension at each age.1) 60 years2) 70 years​


Answer:

1.) 30 years of service

2.)  20 years of service

Step-by-step explanation:

Age at which an employee can retire with full pension formula:

A + B = 90

Where:

A = employee's ageB = number of years of service

To find the years of service (B) given the age of employee (A), substitute the given values for age to A, then solve for  B:

Formula:  A + B = 90

Substitute:

1) 60 years;  A = 60

(60) + B = 90

B = 90 - 60

B = 30

The years of service for retirement required is 30 years for age 60 years of an employee to get a full retirement pension.

2) 70 years;  A = 70

(70) + B = 90

B = 90 - 70

B = 20

The years of service for retirement required is 20 years for age 70 years of an employee to get a full retirement pension.

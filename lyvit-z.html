3. What is levitating in science or gravity?


Answer:

Levitation (from Latin levis, light) is the process by which an object, here a sample, is suspended against gravity, in a stable position, by a force, without physical contact. All that is required on earth for levitation is a force vertically upwards equal to the object's weight.

14. what is the factor of a2-2a-15 ... the first 2(number) is small and slightly levitated thanksss


Answer:

(a-5)(a+3)

Step-by-step explanation:

Let (a+r) and (a+s) be the two factors of the equation a² - 2a - 15

Expanding (a+r)(a+s),

[tex] (a+r) (a+s) = {a}^{2}+(r+s)a+rs={a}^{2}-2a-15[/tex]

In order for a² + (r+s)a + rs be equal to the given equation, (r+s)a = -2a and rs = -15

[tex](r + s)a = - 2a[/tex]

[tex](r + s) = - 2[/tex]

We can see that the sum of roots is -2 while the product of roots is -15

We can factor -15

-15 = (-3×5), (-5×3)

Then by getting their sum we have

5 + (-3) = 2 -5 + 3 = -2

Then we have (a-5)(a+3)

30. In the parable, the Levite was the one who treated the victim with mercy and compassion. true or false ?​


Answer:

False.

Explanation:

In the Parable of The Good Samaritan, the Levite came and looked at him and passed by on the other side. It is the Samaritan who treated him with mercy and had compassion for him.

"But a certain Samaritan, as he journeyed, came where he was: and when he saw him, he had compassion on him, And went to him, and bound up his wounds, pouring oil and wine, and set him on his own beast, and brought him to an inn, and took care of him." (Luke 10:33-34)

There is an irreconcilable difference between Jews and Samaritans because they rejected the writings of the Prophets and all Jewish traditions, and this is the reason why Jews regarded them as the worst of the human race. But in this parable, the man who gave compassion to the victim is a Samaritan.

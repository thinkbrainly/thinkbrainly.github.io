15. Private accounting, as a sub-discipline of accounting, includes: Select one: a. Tax consultancy b. Managerial advisory services c. External auditing d. Management Accounting


Answer:

D. MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING

Explanation:

MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING

        Management Accounting or Cost Accounting is a branch of accounting that is only concern in internal decision-making of the company or client. Accountants in this field do not have connections with the investors or lenders. In this branch, accountants are responsible for identifying, counting, analyzing, and interpreting of the accounts that would help the company or client's management of operations of their business.

       The accountants must use different tools in order to analyze and interpret the data they gathered in the account's information.

Methods of Management Accounting:Margin Analysis - this method is concern in the increase of the production.Capital Budgeting - in this method, accountants must consider the capital expenditures to make new capital budgeting decisions by calculating the NPV or Net Present Value and the IRR or Internal Rate of Return.

25. Public accounting, as a sub-discipline of accounting, includes:Select one:a. Cost accountingb. Management accountingc. Internal auditingd. Management advisory services ​


PUBLIC ACCOUNTING

Public accounting, as a sub-discipline of accounting, includes management advisory services (D). ​

What is Public Accounting?

A company that offers accounting services to other businesses is referred to as public accounting. Public accountants offer their clients tax, auditing, and accounting skills. Most of the time, these services fit into one of the categories listed below.

Differences between Public and Private Accounting

Below is a list of the key distinctions between the two:

PAFs are used by public accounting to offer its clients financial services. Using a company-employed accountant for private accounting means using their services.Private accounting is an internal function of the business, whereas CPAs (certified public accountants) are frequently employed by PAFs, but private accountants are not required to obtain this designation.PAFs manage every aspect of their client's finances, including audit, taxation, consulting, etc. However, private accountant is limited to carrying out the tasks that have been given to them (like preparing budgets, making journal entries, reconciling accounts, etc.). A public accountant in this situation has a variety of experiences that a private accountant would not have.While private accountants often work standard office hours and don't travel much, public accountants frequently travel for business initiatives and put in long hours.

Services

There is a wide range of services that public accounting firms often offer. Let's give some of the most well-known examples.

BookkeepingBank reconciliationAccounts receivable and payableTax accountingPayroll processingForensic AccountingChartered accountingFinancial controller servicesAccounting audit

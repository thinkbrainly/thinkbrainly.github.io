10. A contractor has 3½ sheets of 4 by 8ft plywood. how many square feet of plywood are there?​


Answer:

112 square feet

#BRAINLYFAST

Step-by-step explanation:

Question:

A contractor has 3½ sheets of 4 by 8ft plywood. how many square feet of plywood are there?

Given:

1 Plywood - 4 feet by 8 feet

Total sheets: 3 and 1/2 or 3.5 sheets

In this problem, we have to get the total area, in square feet, of plywood that the contractor has. So to start, we will first get the area of 1 sheet of 4 feet by 8 feet of plywood.

The total area per sheet of plywood can be solved by using the formula in getting the area of a rectangle:

A = L x W

Where

A = Area of a rectangle

L = Length of the rectangle

W = Width of the rectangle

Substituting in the given values, we now have:

A = 4(8) = 32 square feet per sheet of plywood

Since we have 3 and 1/2 or 3.5 sheets of plywood, we multiply 32 by 3.5:

Total area of plywood (in square feet) = 3.5(32) = 112 square feet.

Therefore, the contractor has 112 square feet of plywood in total.

16. Balls with worn-off fuzz becomesAnswer that will eventually affect its flight and the player's control on it. (tennis)


Answer:

They become very light

Explanation:

Tennis balls with worn-off fuzz becomes very light that will eventually affect its flight and the player's control on it.  The fuzz is also otherwise known as the nap. It can change the speed of the ball and it also helps in spinning it as it travels through the air. This can refer to aerodynamic friction. Very little fuzz means less friction with the air.

The fuzz or nap is made of textile materials from wool, nylon and cotton. It is then cut to to shape and then glued over the tennis ball.  Most players using the ball for the first time in a tournament usually feel and examine them first as they look for a ball with perfect and intact fuzz.

17. where are most of the controls for Microsoft word​


Answer:

Content controls are individual controls that you can add and customize for use in templates, forms, and documents. For example, many online forms are designed with a drop-down list control that provides a restricted set of choices for the user of the form. Content controls can provide instructional text for users, and you can set controls to disappear when users type in their own text.

For detailed information about content controls, including descriptions an instructions for each type of control, see Create forms that users complete or print in Word.

You can find the content controls on the Developer tab.

View the Developer tab to add or change content controls

Click File > Options > Customize Ribbon.

In the list of tabs under Customize the Ribbon, select the Developer box and then click OK.

Do one of the following:

To add a control, click in the document where you want to add it, and then click the control you want to add on the Design tab.

To set properties on a control or change an existing control, on the Developer tab, in the Controls group, click Properties.

24. What are the three primary flight control surfaces of an aircraft? Describe each and their function.​


Answer:

The primary flight control surfaces on a fixed-wing aircraft include: ailerons, elevators, and the rudder. The ailerons are attached to the trailing edge of both wings and when moved, rotate the aircraft around the longitudinal axis. The elevator is attached to the trailing edge of the horizontal stabilizer.

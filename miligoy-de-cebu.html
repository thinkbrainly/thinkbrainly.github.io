1. Uyaoy Dance is an Ifugao wedding festival dance accompanied by gongs and is performed by the affluent to attain the second level of the wealthy class. Wealthy people who have performed this dance are entitled to the use of gongs at their death.

2. Kuratsa is a dance of flirtation and courtship, designed to be danced with a partner. Its movements mimic the mating ritual of a rooster and hen. Alcedo said that different versions of the dance could be found in north and central Philippines.

3. Singkíl is a folk dance of the Maranao people of Lake Lanao depicting one of the episodes in the epic poem Darangen, which was popularised by the Bayanihan Philippine National Folk Dance Company.

4. Miligoy de Cebu dance is a dance that originated just north of Cebu City in the Philippines. It usually performed during weddings, baptisms, and special programs. Dancers hold a pair of bamboo castanets in each hand.

5. Tinikling is a traditional Philippine folk dance which originated during the Spanish colonial era. The dance involves two people beating, tapping, and sliding bamboo poles on the ground and against each other in coordination with one or more dancers who step over and in between the poles in a dance.

12. Pit Senyor Santo Niño de Cebu Sulog One beat, One Dance, One Vision​


Answer:

"Pit Senyor" is a phrase commonly used during the Sinulog Festival in Cebu City, Philippines. It is a chant in honor of the Santo Niño de Cebu, which means "Hail, Lord Senior Santo Niño!" "Sulog" means "current" or "flow," and it refers to the strong current that flows through the channels of Cebu's Pahina River.

"One beat, One Dance, One Vision" is a tagline that represents the unity and solidarity of the people of Cebu during the Sinulog Festival. It symbolizes the idea that despite the diversity of cultures and backgrounds, everyone comes together to celebrate the same thing, which is the devotion to the Santo Niño de Cebu. During the festival, people dance to the beat of the drums and chant "Pit Senyor" as a sign of reverence and devotion to the Holy Child.

15. Sino ang pinuno ng Cebu na tumangging magpasakop kay Miguel Lopez de Legazpi?


Answer:

Miguel López de Legazpi

Explanation:

Miguel López de Legazpi also known as El Adelantado and El Viejo (The Elder), was a Spanish navigator and governor who established the first Spanish settlement in the East Indies when his expedition crossed the Pacific Ocean from the Viceroyalty of New Spain in modern-day Mexico, arriving in Cebu in the Philippine Islands in 1565. He was the first Governor-General of the Spanish East Indies, which was governed and mainly located in the Philippines. It also encompassed other Pacific islands namely Guam and the Mariana Islands. After obtaining peace with various indigenous nations and kingdoms, he made Cebu City the capital of the Spanish East Indies in 1565 and later transferred to Manila in 1571.

Ang kolonisasyon at paninirahan ng mga Espanyol ay nagsimula sa pagdating ng ekspedisyon ni Miguel López de Legazpi noong 1565 at itinatag ang unang permanenteng pamayanan ng San Miguel sa isla ng Cebu.

29. Rahang nagtaboy sa grupo ni Miguel Lopez De Legaspi sa Cebu




Si Rajah Tupas (nabinyagan bilang Felipe Tupás, c. 1497 - c. 1565) ang huling Rajah ng Cebu sa Pre-Hispanic Philippines. Siya ang anak ni Sri Parang ang Limp, at ang pamangkin ni Rajah Humabon. Siya ay kilala na nabautismuhan noong Marso 21, 1568 sa edad na 70, na naglalagay ng kanyang kapanganakan noong mga 1497. Pinamahalaan niya ang Cebu sa kanyang mga kasamahan hanggang sa siya ay natalo ng mga sundalo ni Miguel López de Legazpi noong Abril 27, 1565. Sa 4 Hunyo 1565, pinirmahan ni Tupas at Legaspi ang Treaty of Cebu, na epektibong nagbigay sa Espanya ng suzerainty sa Cebu.

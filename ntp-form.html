Introductory Concept Common communicable diseases are those diseases that can be easily spread from person to person through various ways such as direct contact, airborne transmission, or through contaminated food or water. The signs and symptoms of these diseases can vary depending on the type of disease and the affected individual. Some common communicable diseases include the flu, measles, chickenpox, and tuberculosis.

LEARNING ACTIVITY SHEET No. 3-COMPARISON OF COMMUNICABLE AND NON-COMMUNICABLE DISEASES

Introductory Concept

Communicable diseases are those that can be spread from person to person or from animals to humans. Non-communicable diseases, on the other hand, are not caused by infectious agents and cannot be spread from one person to another. While both types of diseases can have serious health consequences, they differ in their causes and modes of transmission.

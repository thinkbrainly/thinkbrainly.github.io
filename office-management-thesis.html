5. Difference between thesis and thesis statement


Answer:

Your thesis is the main idea, point, incident, or argument of your paper. That is in your mind.

Your thesis statement is that idea expressed in a sentence. An important caveat: this sentence must conform to the class or the reviewer’s (professor’s) requirement. If there is a specific form required, that should have been explained to you and taught to you before you have to write it. In one of my classes, stdents were required to create thesis statements, bring them to class, and have the critiqued in class discussions. We also studied examples of both good and bad thesis statements from published papers.

Thesis — idea.

Thesis statement — a sentence giving the thesis (idea) which conforms to the established form stating that idea.

26. Doc is studying to be a police officer. In the middle of his written test, he is asked to write a short essay (800 words). He has to write about a danger that he sees that most police officers do not think about. Which of the following would make a good thesis for Doc's essay?


Answer:

A good thesis for Doc's essay could be: "Police officers often face many dangers in the course of their work, but one danger that is often overlooked is the risk of [insert danger here]. This danger is [insert explanation of how it is overlooked or underestimated] and it is important for police officers to be aware of it in order to protect themselves and the communities they serve."

Some examples of dangers that Doc could write about could be the risk of exposure to hazardous substances, the danger of mental health issues such as burnout or PTSD, or the risk of violence from people who may not be perceived as traditional threats. Whatever danger Doc chooses to write about, it should be one that he feels is important for police officers to be aware of and that he can provide evidence and examples to support.

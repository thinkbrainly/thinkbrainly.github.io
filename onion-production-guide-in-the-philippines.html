1. Considering the price surge of onions, why is onion production important in the Philippines? Why do we need to improve onion production? (Palagay po mga sources ninyo ty)


Answer:

Onion production is important in the Philippines for several reasons:

1. Food security: Onions are a staple ingredient in many dishes in the Philippines, and producing a sufficient supply of onions can help ensure food security for the country's growing population.

2. Economic benefits: The production and export of onions can provide income and job opportunities for farmers, especially in rural areas, and contribute to the overall growth of the economy.

3. Reduction of dependence on imports: By increasing onion production, the Philippines can reduce its dependence on imports, which can be subject to price fluctuations and supply disruptions.

4. Improved quality of life: With increased income and access to locally-grown onions, farmers and their families can enjoy a better quality of life.

To improve onion production in the Philippines, there are several steps that can be taken, such as:

1. Adoption of modern farming techniques: The use of modern farming techniques, such as precision agriculture and integrated pest management, can help farmers improve their yields and reduce costs.

2. Provision of technical assistance and training: Providing farmers with training and technical assistance on best practices for onion production can help them increase their yields and improve the quality of their produce.

3. Investment in research and development: Investing in research and development of new varieties of onions that are better adapted to local conditions and have improved yields and disease resistance can help increase the overall productivity of the sector.

4. Infrastructure development: Improving infrastructure, such as roads, storage facilities, and marketing networks, can help farmers get their produce to market more efficiently and increase their income.

Sources:

Department of Agriculture, Republic of the Philippines. (n.d.). Onion. Retrieved from http://www.da.gov.ph/crops/onion.

Explanation:

Most of the cell parts (called organelles) are almost invisible because they are colorless. We also looked at the cells of an onion bulb. Since the onion bulb grows underground, it doesn't see any sunlight and so it doesn't have any chloroplasts for photosynthesis.

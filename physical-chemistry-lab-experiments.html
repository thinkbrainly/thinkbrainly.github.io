8. why you should tie your long hair back when working in a chemistry lab? ​


ANSWER:

It is important to tie back long hair when working in a chemistry lab for several reasons:

• Safety: Chemicals can be hazardous and may react with hair or get caught in it. Tying back long hair can help prevent it from coming into contact with chemicals or getting caught in equipment.

• Hygiene: Long hair can also be a source of contamination in a lab, as it may shed and spread bacteria or other microorganisms. By tying hair back, it helps to prevent contamination and maintain a sterile environment.

• Compliance: Many lab safety regulations and guidelines require employees to tie back their hair if it is long. Following these regulations helps ensure the safety of all lab personnel and visitors.

Why is physical chemistry important?

Physical chemistry is essential to understanding the other branches of chemistry. It provides a basis for understanding the thermodynamic influences (principally, the entropy changes accompanying reactions) that drive chemical reactions forward

Physical chemistry is a part of our everyday life. The batteries in our vehicles use the principle of electrochemistry. The photosynthetic process of green plants is an example of physical chemistry applied by biological systems. Solar devices change energy from sunlight into electrical energy.

Methanol, Ethanol, Acetone, Xylene, Toluene, Ethyl acetate, Tetrahydrofuran, Ethyl ether, Benzene, Dimethylformamide, Acetonitrile, Hexane, Pyridine, etc.  

4. Basic flammable liquids:

Flammable amines such as: Triethylamine, Diethylamine, TEMED, Ethylaminediamine,

5. Inorganic bases:

Metal hydroxides such as Sodium, Potassium, Calcium, Nickel hydroxide Ammonium hydroxide,  

6. Organic bases:

Amines such as  Ethanolamine, Tributylamine, etc.  

Acidic Flammable Liquids:

Glacial acetic acid (100%), Acetic acid (>80%), Acetic anhydride, Formic acid (>85%), Propanoic acid (100%) (also called Propionic acid)

Butyric acid, Pentanoic acid, etc.  

8. Inorganic acids:

Hydrochloric acid, Sulfuric acid, Phosphoric acid, Hydrofluoric Acid, etc

Many are in aqueous solution, but they are also available as mixtures or pure compounds, such  as  Acrylamide, Formaldehyde, Glutaraldehyde, Chloroform, Phenol, Methylene chloride

10. Cyanides

Sodium cyanide, Potassium cyanide, Calcium cyanide

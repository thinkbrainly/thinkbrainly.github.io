Given:

Required:

Amount of poles in the pile

Step 3: Solve for the amount of poles in the pile.

Solution:

The formula for the sum of the arithmetic sequence is:

S = [n (a1 + an)]/2

where S - sum of the arithmetic sequence

n - number/order of terms

a1 - first term

an - nth term, (a1, a2, a3, ... , an)

The value of n is unknown so we should first find it by using the formula for nth term of an arithmetic series is given by:

an = a1 +d(n - 1)

where: an - nth term, (a1, a2, a3, ... , an)

a1 - first term

d - common difference

n - number/order of terms

The value of an is unknown, let's assume that an = 1 or at the nth term the number of cans is one. The common difference is -1 so we can assume that in the last row, there are only 1 pole in the last row. Substitute the value of an, d and a1 to find the value of n.

1 = 50 + [-1(n -1)]

1 = 50 - n + 1

n = 50

S = [50(50 + 1)]/2

S = [(50)(51)]/2

22. In an electrical circuit, electrons move from the ______A. Negative pole to negative poleB. Postive pole to negative poleC. Positive pole to positive pole​


B. Positive pole to negative pole

The flow of electrons around the circuit is opposite to the direction of the conventional current flow being negative to positive.

The actual current flowing in an electrical circuit is composed of electrons that flow from the negative pole of the battery (the cathode) and return to the positive pole (the anode) of the battery.

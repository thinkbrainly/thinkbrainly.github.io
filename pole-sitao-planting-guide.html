Thigmotropism is the kind of tropism that the plant reacts to. The directed growth in response to a stimulus is known as a tropism. A plant (or a portion of a plant) exhibits positive tropism when it grows in the direction of the stimulus, and a plant exhibits negative tropism when it grows away from the stimulus. Thigmotropism is a directional growth movement that develops in response to a touch stimulation through the mechanosensory system.

The induction of some pattern of differential growth typically results in this directional reaction. Some plants, like the sweet pea, have climbing tendrils that serve as a clear illustration of this phenomena. For instance, vines contain tiny tendrils that wrap around trellises or walls. Typically, plants like this don't have robust stems. Thigmotropism hence aids in maintaining their balance and growth.

Thigmotropism is the process by which plants migrate to grow around an object that is subjected to continuous directional pressure, such as a trellis. Thigmonastic reactions to touch include the opening and closing of leaves, petals, or other elements of the plant. The creeping plant grows and coils around the tree as it grows upwards toward it due to thigmotropism activity. Ivies frequently use trees as a climbing aid.

20. In an electrical circuit, electrons move from the ______A. Negative pole to negative poleB. Postive pole to negative poleC. Positive pole to positive pole​


B. Positive pole to negative pole

The flow of electrons around the circuit is opposite to the direction of the conventional current flow being negative to positive.

The actual current flowing in an electrical circuit is composed of electrons that flow from the negative pole of the battery (the cathode) and return to the positive pole (the anode) of the battery.

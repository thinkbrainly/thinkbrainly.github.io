27. A new shopping mall is gaining in popularity. Every day since it opened, the number of shoppers is 20%, percent more than the number of shoppers the day before. The total number of shoppers over the first 4 days is 671


Answer:

There are 125 shoppers on the first day.

There are 150 shoppers on the second day.

There are 180 shoppers on the third day.

There are 216 shoppers on the fourth day.

Further Explanation

The problem is a bit tricky. The clue here is that you need to understand the situation in the problem. The scenario is like this, for the first day of the shopping, there is x number of shoppers. On the second day, the number of shoppers have increased by 20% of the previous day. On the third day, the shoppers have increased by 20% of the previous day. Finally, on the fourth, it has increased again by the same percentage of the previous day. The accumulated number of shoppers from the first to the fourth day is 671.

Construct a mathematical model based on the scenario discussed above. From the mathematical model being defined, solve the value of the unknown variable applying the appropriate mathematical laws.

Constructing the Mathematical Model

1. Set an unknown variable.

2. Construct the model based on the situation in the problem.

3. Solve for the unknown variable.

4. Write a conclusion about the value.

Solution:

Let [tex]x[/tex] be the number of shoppers for the first day.

First Day

              [tex]x[/tex] numbers of shoppers

Second Day (20% more of the previous day)

              [tex]x+0.20x=1.20x[/tex]

Third Day (20% more of the previous day)

             [tex]0.20(1.20x)+1.20x=0.24x+1.20x=1.44x[/tex]

Fourth Day

             [tex]0.20(1.44x)+1.44x=0.288x+1.44x=1.728x[/tex]

Equate the sum of the shoppers of each day to 671, then solve for [tex]x[/tex].

                  [tex]\begin{aligned}x+1.20x+1.44x+1.728x&=671\\5.368x&=671\\\frac{5.368x}{5.368}&=\frac{671}{5.368}\\x&=125\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that the number of shoppers on the first day is 125. The number of shoppers for the second day is 20% more of the first day, so it's [tex]125+0.20(125)=150[/tex]. The number of shoppers for the third day is 20% more of the second day, so  it's [tex]150+0.20(150)=180[/tex]. The number of shoppers for the fourth day is 20%  more than the third day, so it's [tex]180+0.20(180)=216[/tex]. The total shoppers over the first four days are [tex]125+150+180+216=671[/tex].

28. A new shopping mall is gaining in popularity. Every day since it opened, the number of shoppers is 20% more than the number of shoppers the day before. The total number of shoppers over the first 4 days is 671. How many shoppers were at the mall on the first day?


Answer:

538

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a geometric sequence and sum problem.

Solve for the value of [tex]a_{1}[/tex] using geometric sum formula

[tex]S_{n} = \frac{a_{1}(1-r^{n})}{1-r}[/tex]

Step 1: Substitute:

[tex]671=\frac{a_{1}(1-0.20^{4})}{1-0.20}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for [tex]a_{1}[/tex] (first day)

[tex]671 = \frac{a_{1}(1-1.6x10x^{-3})}{0.80}[/tex]

[tex](0.80)(671) = {a_{1}(0.9984)[/tex]

[tex]\frac{(0.80)(671)}{(0.9984)}=a_{1}[/tex]

[tex]a_{1} = 537.66[/tex]

round up to 538

Therefore, there were 538 people on the first day.

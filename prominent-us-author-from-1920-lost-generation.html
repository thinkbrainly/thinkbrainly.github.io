18. during the modern times why has paper mache rapidly lost its initial prominence​


Paper mache is a craft that involves shaping and molding paper pulp mixed with glue or paste to create various decorative or functional objects. It has been used for centuries to create a wide range of items such as masks, figurines, and decorative boxes.

During modern times, paper mache has lost some of its initial prominence due to several factors. One of the main reasons is the emergence of new materials and technologies that are cheaper and easier to work with than paper mache. For example, plastic molding has become more prevalent in the manufacturing of various decorative and functional items.

Another reason for the decline in popularity of paper mache is the shift in consumer preferences towards more sleek and modern designs. Paper mache has a traditional and rustic feel, which may not appeal to everyone. As people's tastes change, they may be less interested in paper mache objects and more drawn to modern materials and designs.

Furthermore, the mass production of decorative items has also contributed to the decline in paper mache's prominence. The demand for cheaper and quicker manufacturing methods has led to the use of automated machines and molds, which can produce objects at a faster rate and at a lower cost than handcrafted paper mache objects.

Overall, while paper mache continues to be used in various craft projects and artistic endeavors, its prominence has declined in modern times due to the emergence of new materials and technologies, changing consumer preferences, and the mass production of decorative items.

26. Mr. Gamrod is selling live chicken for a living. Due to Covid-19, their barangay was declared to be a high-risk area and a lockdown was implemented. Mr. Gamrod, knowing he is the only one selling chicken in their barangay decided to add P20 pesos to the price of his chickens every time a chicken will be bought. If Mr.G amrod has 20 chickens left and he sells them for the initial price of P160 each, how much will the last chicken cost if all of the other 19 was bought?


Answer:

P540.

Step-by-step explanation:

This is an arithmetic series progression having a common difference between two consecutive terms

To get the last term of the arithmetic sequence

[tex]a_{n} = a_{1} +d(n-1)\\[/tex]

Where

[tex]a_{1}[/tex] is the first term which is 160

[tex]a_{n}[/tex] is the last term

n is the number of terms which is 20

d is the common difference between two consecutive terms which is 20

Substituting the given values

[tex]a_{n} = a_{1} +d(n-1)\\[/tex]

[tex]a_{n} = 160 +20(20-1)\\\\a_{n} = 540[/tex]

The last chicken will cost P540.

12. if it take mark 13 minutes to swim 2 laps, how long will it to swim 15 laps? What are given facts? ​


Answer:

Given facts:

Mark can swim 2 laps in 13 minutes.

We can use proportion to find how long it will take Mark to swim 15 laps:

If 2 laps take 13 minutes, then 1 lap takes 13/2 = 6.5 minutes.

Therefore, 15 laps will take:

15 laps × 6.5 minutes/lap = 97.5 minutes

So it will take Mark 97.5 minutes (or 1 hour and 37.5 minutes) to swim 15 laps.

25. if it takes mark 13 minutes to swim 2 laps, How long will it to swim 15 laps? what is Solution? ​


Answer:

We can use proportional reasoning to solve this problem.

If Mark swims 2 laps in 13 minutes, we can set up a proportion to find out how long it would take him to swim 15 laps:

2 laps / 13 minutes = 15 laps / x minutes

where x is the number of minutes it would take Mark to swim 15 laps.

To solve for x, we can cross-multiply:

2 laps * x minutes = 13 minutes * 15 laps

2x = 195

x = 97.5

Therefore, it would take Mark 97.5 minutes to swim 15 laps.

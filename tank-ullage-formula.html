Total volume of a cylinder shaped tank is the area, A, of the circular end times the length, l. A = πr2 where r is the radius which is equal to 1/2 the diameter or d/2. Therefore:

V(tank) = πr2l

Total volume of a cylinder shaped tank is the area, A, of the circular end times the height, h. A = πr2 where r is the radius which is equal to d/2. Therefore:

V(tank) = πr2h

The filled volume of a vertical cylinder tank is just a shorter cylinder with the same radius, r, and diameter, d, but height is now the fill height or f.  Therefore:

V(fill) = πr2f

20. A water tank that is rectangular in shape is 15.5 feet long, 12.3 feet wide, and 6 feet deep. What is the volume and surface area of the water tank? With formula po


Answer:

Volume = length x width x depth

Substituting the given values, we get:

Volume = 15.5 ft x 12.3 ft x 6 ft = 1150.1 cubic feet

Therefore, the volume of the water tank is 1150.1 cubic feet.

To find the surface area, we need to calculate the area of each face and then add them up.

The tank has six faces: a top and a bottom, two long sides, and two short ends.

The area of the top and bottom faces are the same and is given by the product of the length and width:

Area of top/bottom face = length x width

Substituting the given values, we get:

Area of top/bottom face = 15.5 ft x 12.3 ft = 190.65 square feet

The area of the two long sides is the same and is given by the product of the height and width:

Area of long sides = height x width

Substituting the given values, we get:

Area of long sides = 6 ft x 12.3 ft = 73.8 square feet

The area of the two short ends is the same and is given by the product of the height and length:

Area of short ends = height x length

Substituting the given values, we get:

Area of short ends = 6 ft x 15.5 ft = 93 square feet

Now, we can add up all the face areas to get the total surface area:

Total surface area = 2 x Area of top/bottom face + 2 x Area of long sides + 2 x Area of short ends

Substituting the calculated values, we get:

Total surface area = 2 x 190.65 sq ft + 2 x 73.8 sq ft + 2 x 93 sq ft = 755.1 square feet

Therefore, the surface area of the water tank is 755.1 square feet.

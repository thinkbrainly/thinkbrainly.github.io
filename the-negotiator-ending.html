28. what is negotiating rate​


Answer:

Negotiating rate is a term used in the finance and banking industries to refer to the interest rate that a borrower and a lender agree upon in a loan or credit agreement after negotiating and considering various factors, such as the borrower's creditworthiness, the loan amount, and the loan term. Negotiating rate is often different from the initial or advertised rate because it takes into account the borrower's specific financial situation and the lender's risk assessment. A borrower who has a good credit score and a stable income may be able to negotiate a lower interest rate than someone with a lower credit score or less income. The negotiating rate is an important component of a loan or credit agreement because it determines the total cost of borrowing for the borrower, including the interest charges, fees, and other costs.

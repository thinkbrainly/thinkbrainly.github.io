9. Edna paid 1200 for a pair of pants and a blouse.the cost of the pair of pants is 600 more than the cost of the blouse


Answer:

The cost of the blouse is 300c. The cost of the pair of pants is 900c.

Step-by-step explanation:

*For now, I'll give the currency the variable c. If there is a specified currency in the problem (e.g. dollars, yen, etc.), feel free to replace the c's (excluding c(b) and c(p)) in the solution with the said currency.

Let the cost of the blouse = c(b) and the cost of the pair of pants = c(p):

1,200c = c(b) + c(p)

c(p) = c(b) + 600c. Therefore:

1,200c = c(b) + [c(b) + 600c]

1,200c - 600c = 2c(b)

[tex]\frac{2c(b)}{2}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{600c}{2}[/tex]

c(b) = 300c ; c(p) = 300c + 600c = 900c

Are these the desired answers?

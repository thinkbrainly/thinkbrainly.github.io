10. read patiently the summary of the story of keesh by Jack London​


Answer:

SHEP O'NEAL: Keesh lived at the edge of the polar sea. He had seen thirteen suns in the Eskimo way of keeping time. Among the Eskimos, the sun each winter leaves the land in darkness. And the next year, a new sun returns, so it might be warm again.

The father of Keesh had been a brave man. But he had died hunting for food. Keesh was his only son. Keesh lived along with his mother, Ikeega.

One night, the village council met in the big igloo of Klosh-kwan, the chief. Keesh was there with the others. He listened, then waited for silence.

He said, “It is true that you give us some meat. But it is often old and tough meat, and has many bones.”

The hunters were surprised. This was a child speaking against them. A child talking like a grown man!

Keesh said, “My father, Bok, was a great hunter. It is said that Bok brought home more meat than any of the two best hunters. And that he divided the meat so that all got an equal share.”

“Naah! Naah!” the hunters cried. “Put the child out! Send him to bed. He should not talk to gray-beards this way!”

Keesh waited until the noise stopped. “You have a wife, Ugh-gluk,” he said. “And you speak for her. My mother has no one but me. So I speak. As I say, Bok hunted greatly, but is now dead. It is only fair then that my mother, who was his wife, and I, his son, should have meat when the tribe has meat. I, Keesh, son of Bok, have spoken.”

Again, there was a great noise in the igloo. The council ordered Keesh to bed. It even talked of giving him no food.

Keesh jumped to his feet. “Hear me!” he cried. “Never shall I speak in the council igloo again. I shall go hunt meat like my father, Bok.”

There was much laughter when Keesh spoke of hunting. The laughter followed Keesh as he left the council meeting.

The next day, Keesh started out for the shore, where the land meets the ice. Those who watched saw that he carried his bow and many arrows. Across his shoulder was his father’s big hunting spear. Again there was laughter.

One day passed, then a second. On the third day, a great wind blew. There was no sign of Keesh. His mother, Ikeega, put burned seal oil on her face to show her sorrow.

Introduction

Keesh is a short story written by Jack London in 1905. It is set in the Arctic and tells the story of a young Inuit boy named Keesh who is struggling to survive in a harsh environment. The story follows Keesh as he faces many challenges, including starvation, extreme weather conditions, and hostile animals. He must rely on his wits and courage to survive and eventually succeeds in becoming the leader of his tribe.

Summary

Keesh is an Inuit boy living in the Arctic with his tribe. He is brave and resourceful, but he is also very young and inexperienced. One day, while out hunting, Keesh encounters a polar bear that nearly kills him. He manages to escape with his life but loses all of his weapons in the process. With no way to hunt for food, Keesh must rely on his wits to survive. He eventually finds an abandoned seal carcass which he uses to feed himself and his tribe.

Keesh's bravery and resourcefulness soon earn him respect from the other members of his tribe. When their leader dies, they choose Keesh as their new leader despite his young age. With the help of his tribe, Keesh leads them through many difficult times until they eventually find a new home where they can live peacefully.

Critical Evaluation

Keesh is an inspiring story about courage and perseverance in the face of adversity. It shows how even someone as young as Keesh can rise above difficult circumstances and become a leader if they have enough determination and courage. The story also highlights how important it is for people to work together if they want to succeed in difficult situations like those faced by Keesh's tribe.

The story also provides an interesting insight into Inuit culture at the time it was written (1905). It shows how Inuits relied on their wits and courage to survive in such a harsh environment, as well as how important family ties were for them at that time.

Conclusion

Keesh is an inspiring story about courage, perseverance, and teamwork set against a backdrop of extreme hardship in the Arctic wilderness. It provides an interesting insight into Inuit culture at the time it was written (1905) while also highlighting how important it is for people to work together if they want to succeed in difficult situations like those faced by Keesh's tribe.

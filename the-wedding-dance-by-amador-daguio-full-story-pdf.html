"The Wedding Dance" Plot Diagram Example

Exposition

The setting is a mountain village of the Philippines where Awiyao has been remarried.

Conflict

Awiyao has left his wife Lumnay, whom he loved very much. However, she couldn’t give him a child. He has now married Madulimay in hopes to have a son, who will continue his legacy. This is something Awiyao expresses as important in his culture. However, Lumnay is upset because she loves Awiyao and doesn’t want this separation.

Rising action

Outside, the villagers are dancing in celebration of the wedding. Awiyao leaves to try and comfort Lumnay. He offers her many items of the life that they built together. Lumnay refuses them and clings to Awiyao, wishing he would stay.

Climax

Awiyao finally leaves to re-join the wedding and Lumnay runs into the hills.

Falling Action

Lumnay sits on the side of the mountain overlooking the blazing fire and dancing women, thinking about how her life has changed. She has a sense of desperation, isolation, and worthlessness.

Resoultion

The reader is left not knowing what will become of Lumnay.

Awiyao goes back to the wedding, to the wedding dance, after being fetched by some friends. Lumnay wants to follow, partly because of the dance, and partly because she wants to put a stop to their tribe’s tradition of having to marry another partner just to have a child.

In terms of current events at the time of publication, the Philippines was still recovering from World War II and undergoing significant social and political changes. The country was transitioning from being a colony of the United States to an independent nation. This transition brought about new ideas and perspectives on traditional practices and beliefs.

Overall, "The Wedding Dance" provides a glimpse into the cultural practices of the Ifugao people and highlights the struggles faced by women in traditional societies. 28. literary selection critique in the wedding dance by amador daguio ​


"The Wedding Dance" by Amador Daguio is a short story that explores the complexities of love and cultural traditions. The story follows Awiyao and Lumnay, a married couple from the Kalinga tribe in the Philippines, who are struggling to conceive a child. In an effort to fulfill his desire for a son, Awiyao decides to leave Lumnay and seek out another wife. The story depicts the emotional turmoil that both characters experience as they confront the reality of their situation.

One of the strengths of this story is its portrayal of the cultural practices and traditions of the Kalinga tribe. Daguio effectively captures the nuances of their customs and beliefs, such as the importance of fertility and the role of the dance in courtship. This attention to detail gives the story a sense of authenticity and depth.

Another strength of the story is the way it portrays the emotional complexity of the characters. Awiyao is torn between his love for Lumnay and his desire for a son, while Lumnay struggles with feelings of abandonment and heartbreak. These conflicting emotions are depicted with sensitivity and nuance, making the characters feel like real people with real struggles.

One potential weakness of the story is its abrupt ending. The story ends with Lumnay joining in the wedding dance of Awiyao and his new wife, leaving the reader to wonder about the characters' fates. While this open-endedness can be effective in some cases, it may leave some readers feeling unsatisfied or confused.

Overall, "The Wedding Dance" is a poignant and beautifully written story that explores the complexities of love, culture, and tradition. It is a story that resonates with readers on a universal level, even as it offers a window into a specific cultural context

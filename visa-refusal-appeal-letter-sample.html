6. LETTER OF APPEAL ABOUT SECOND HAND SMOKING​


Answer:

Dear [Name],

I am writing to appeal to you about the dangers of second-hand smoking. As you may know, second-hand smoke is a serious health hazard that can cause a variety of illnesses and even death. It is especially dangerous for children, who are more vulnerable to the effects of second-hand smoke than adults.

Second-hand smoke contains more than 7,000 chemicals, including hundreds that are toxic and about 70 that can cause cancer. It is estimated that second-hand smoke causes more than 41,000 deaths each year in the United States alone. Children exposed to second-hand smoke are at an increased risk for asthma, bronchitis, pneumonia, ear infections, and other respiratory illnesses. They also have an increased risk of sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS).

The only way to fully protect non-smokers from the dangers of second-hand smoke is to eliminate smoking in all indoor spaces. This includes homes, workplaces, restaurants and bars. I urge you to take action by implementing policies that will protect people from exposure to second-hand smoke in your community or workplace.

Thank you for your time and consideration on this important issue. I look forward to hearing from you soon about how we can work together to reduce the risks associated with second-hand smoke exposure.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]

Answer:

Dear [Recipient],

I am writing this letter to appeal to your sense of responsibility and concern for the well-being of others regarding the issue of second-hand smoking. As you may know, second-hand smoke is a significant public health issue that affects not only smokers but also non-smokers who are exposed to it.

I understand that smoking is a personal choice, but the harmful effects of second-hand smoke cannot be ignored. Exposure to second-hand smoke can lead to various health problems, including respiratory illnesses, heart disease, and even cancer. This is particularly concerning for vulnerable populations, such as children, pregnant women, and individuals with pre-existing health conditions.

As someone who smokes, I appeal to you to be more considerate of those around you. Please refrain from smoking in public areas, especially where there are children or non-smokers present. I also urge you to be mindful of your cigarette butts and dispose of them properly to prevent fires and environmental pollution.

We all have a responsibility to ensure the health and safety of our community, and that includes being aware of the impact our actions have on others. I implore you to take this matter seriously and make a conscious effort to reduce the harmful effects of second-hand smoking.

Solution:

(Number of ways)/(All outcomes)

= 1/3 or 33.33%

The requirements for obtaining a visa vary depending on the type of visa you are applying for and the country you are applying to.

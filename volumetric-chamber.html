2. explain volumetric analysis​


Answer:

Volumetric analysis, any method of quantitative chemical analysis in which the amount of a substance is determined by measuring the volume that it occupies or, in broader usage, the volume of a second substance that combines with the first in known proportions, more correctly called titrimetric analysis.


3. What are the methods of Volumetric analysis?​


Acid-Base titrations, Redox titrations and Complexometric titrations are the major techniques in volumetric analysis.

8. What is the principle of volumetric analysis?​


Answer:

In volumetric analysis the weight of a reagent necessary to complete a chemical reaction is ascertained by determining the volume of a solution containing a known concentration of the reagent required to react stoichiometrically with the element. From this volume the percentage of the element can be calculated.

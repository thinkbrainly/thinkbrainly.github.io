15. write a paragraph about why women washes the dishes​


The tradition of women washing dishes dates back to the time when gender roles were strictly defined in society. It was considered a woman's job to take care of the household chores while men were expected to work outside and provide for the family. The act of washing dishes was seen as a way to show love and care for the family by ensuring that they had clean utensils and plates to eat from. Though gender roles have become less rigid in modern times, the task of washing dishes is still commonly associated with women. However, it is important to recognize that both men and women can and should share household responsibilities equally.

22. Story of why women wash the dishes summary​


Answer:Why Women Wash Dishes is a lighthearted tale about Hugo and Imelda, who are referred to in the story as Ka Ugong and Ka Maldang.They live in a Santa Rosa area. Ka Ugong and Ka Maldang are still squabbling about who will wash the dishes.Ka Maldang would take her broom, and Ka Ugong would have to hide under their tables as the battle escalated.Imelda and Hugo made a bet to see who would wash the dishes for the rest of their lives: whoever spoke first would wash their dishes forever. When the bet began, and their group became aware of the deafening silence, things became more complicated.The neighbors had to call the herb man at one point, believing that one or both of them were possessed by an evil spirit. In contrast to Ka Maldang, who twisted the herb man's finger when he tried to lay it on Ka Maldang's forehead, the herb man tried to do his thing and applied it to Ka Ugong, who did not even bother to move.To avoid further harm to their area, the herb man has decided that the neighbors build a coffin out of bamboos and bury the suspected "bewitched" couple alive.Ka Ugong simply went with the flow and remained still on the day of the burial. When it was Ka Maldang's turn to be lifted, she stood up and yelled at these men to leave their home.Ka Ugong, giddy with delight, dashed out of their home, yelling in delight that he would never have to wash dishes again.

4. For the following reaction, 40.5 grams of sulfuric acid are allowed to react with 37.6 grams of zinc hydroxide . sulfuric acid(aq) + zinc hydroxide(s) zinc sulfate(aq) + water(l)


STOICHIOMETRYCalculations Based on Formulas and Equations

Q: What is the maximum amount of zinc sulfate that can be formed?

SOLUTION

Balanced Equation:

H₂SO₄ + Zn(OH)₂ → ZnSO₄ + 2 H₂O

[tex]40.5 \: g. \: H_{2}SO_{4} \: \times \: \frac{1 \: mol \: \: H_{2}SO_{4}} {98 \: g \:H_{2}SO_{4}} \: \times \: \frac{1 \: mol \: Zn SO_{4}} {1 \: mol \:H_{2}SO_{4}} \: = \: 0.413 \: Zn SO_{4} \\ \\

37.6 \: g. \: Zn(OH)_{2} \: \times \: \frac{1 \: mol \:Zn(OH)_{2}}{99 \: g \: Zn(OH)_{2}} \: \times \frac{1 \: mol \: Zn SO_{4}}{1 \: mol \: Zn(OH)_{2} } \: = \: 0.380 Zn SO_{4} \\ \\

0.380 \: Zn SO_{4} \: + \: \frac{161 \: g \: Zn SO_{4} }{1 \: mol \ \: \: Zn SO_{4} } = \: 61.18 \: g \: Zn SO_{4}[/tex]

Answer: 61.18 ZnSO₄

Q: What is the formula of limiting reagent?

Answer: Zn(OH)₂

Q: What amount of the excess reagent remains after the reaction is complete?

SOLUTION:

[tex]0.380 \: Zn SO_{4} \: \times \: \frac{1 \: mol \: \: H_{2}SO_{4}}{1 \: mol \ \: \: Zn SO_{4} } \times \frac{98 \: g \:H_{2}SO_{4}}{1 \: mol \:H_{2}SO_{4}} = \: 37.24\: g \: H_{2}SO_{4} \\ \\ 37.6 \: g - 37.24 \: g = 0.36 \: g \: in \: excess[/tex]

Answer: 0.36 g. in excess

30. If 500g of zinc was added to a given amount of sulfuric acid, what mass of zinc sulfate may be recovered at the end of the reaction?Zn + H2SO4 ZnSO4 + H2​


Solution:

Note: The chemical equation is already balanced.

According to the balanced equation above, 1 mol Zn is equivalent to 1 mol ZnSO₄

The conversion steps are

grams of Zn → moles of Zn → moles of ZnSO₄ → grams of ZnSO₄

Combine these steps into one equation. Therefore,

[tex]\text{mass of ZnSO₄ = 500 g Zn} × \frac{\text{1 mol Zn}}{\text{65.39 g Zn}} × \frac{\text{1 mol ZnSO₄}}{\text{1 mol Zn}} × \frac{\text{161.46 g ZnSO₄}}{\text{1 mol ZnSO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{\text{mass of ZnSO₄ = 1.23 × 10³ g}}[/tex]
